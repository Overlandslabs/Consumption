OI-46 Confirm how the ARB jump pack is recharged in the field
      NEIL (verify) · before departure · Opened 24 Jul 2026, from the RA-4 ruling
      OPEN — With no spare alternator carried (RA-4 ruled 24 Jul 2026), the
      ARB lithium jump pack is promoted from convenience to CRITICAL PATH.
      R32 is explicit that the alternator is the crank battery's only
      charge source: solar and shore power both serve the house bank, the
      Orion is one-way crank->house, and starting off the LiFePO4 is
      prohibited. So after an alternator failure the jump pack is the only
      way the engine starts, for as long as it takes to reach a
      replacement.
      THE QUESTION: can the pack itself be recharged in the field from the
      house bank — 12 V socket or mains via the MultiPlus — and how long
      does it take? If YES, the survival chain is solar -> house bank ->
      jump pack -> crank, and that chain must be written into R32 as
      doctrine, because at present nobody has stated it anywhere. If NO,
      the ruling leaves a hole that has to be filled another way (a small
      standalone crank maintainer, a second pack, or reinstating the spare
      alternator decision).
      Cheap to answer: read the pack's manual and test it once. Record the
      method and the recharge time in R32.
