00 — Overview

Why This Document Exists

The LC76 has more than 120 individual seals, gaskets and O-rings that can leak. On a 305,000 km expedition vehicle, any one of them can cost you a day, a week, or the whole trip — depending on what it seals and where you are when it fails.

This atlas is not a repair manual. It is a failure map. Each seal is listed with what it seals, how failure presents in the field, and what the operational impact is — so that when you see an oil drip, a weep stain, or a fluid level dropping, you can locate the source quickly and know whether it's a "stop now" or a "monitor and top up" situation.

Cross-reference with R3 (what to carry), R4 (engine component context), R8 (this vehicle's service history), R13 (drivetrain), and R18 (symptom-based troubleshooting).

How to Read the Cards

Each seal card has a priority stripe on its left edge:

Red — Stop driving. Loss of this seal ends the day or damages the vehicle.   Amber — Monitor and top up. You can drive, but plan a fix.   Green — Cosmetic or slow weep. Not urgent.

Scope boundary. This document does not contain torque specs, disassembly procedures, or OEM part numbers. For what to carry, see R3. For how to diagnose by symptom, see R18. For service intervals, see R8.

Ranked by likelihood × consequence given 305,000 km, known front-axle history, SAC turbo, and the 2028 departure horizon. This is the list to inspect at every pre-trip check and at every service.

02 — §1 Engine · Oil-Sealing Surfaces

Engine Oil Seals

The 1HZ has well-known oil-leak zones — most are at the front of the engine (timing case), the valve cover, and the rear main. None are hidden or mysterious; all are documented on the 70 Series forums and all are fixable with common tools. The key is catching them before they become three-fluid cross-contamination events.

1.1  Front Crankshaft Oil Seal
CRITICAL
Behind crank pulley · inside timing cover
What It Seals
Engine oil at the crankshaft nose where the shaft exits the block into the timing case.
Failure Symptom
Oil film down the front of the block, thrown by belt speed onto the radiator core and sump. Often misdiagnosed as a sump gasket leak.
Field Impact
Oil contaminates the timing belt. A contaminated belt fails early and destroys the head (interference engine). Replace with every timing belt — no exceptions.
1.2  Camshaft Front Oil Seal
CRITICAL
Top of timing case · behind cam sprocket
What It Seals
Engine oil at the camshaft nose where it exits the cylinder head.
Failure Symptom
Oil inside the timing cover, weeping out the lower edge and flung forward.
Field Impact
Same as front crank seal — contaminates the timing belt. Replace with every belt change.
1.3  Injection Pump Drive Shaft Seal
CRITICAL
Behind IP sprocket · inside timing case
What It Seals
Engine oil between the timing case and the injection pump body. If this fails inward, oil level in the IP rises over time.
Failure Symptom
Oil drip behind the IP; or rising fuel level in the engine oil if the seal fails the other way.
Field Impact
Often missed on timing belt jobs. Replace with every belt change alongside front crank and cam seals — the three seals are accessed together.
1.4  Timing Cover Gasket / RTV Bead
MONITOR
Between timing cover and block
What It Seals
Engine oil inside the timing case.
Failure Symptom
Weep along the lower cover-to-block seam where oil pools. Drips onto front crossmember.
Field Impact
Usually slow. Replace with timing belt service.
1.5  Oil Pump Cover O-Ring / Shaft Seal
MONITOR
Inside front cover · driven off crank nose
What It Seals
Pressurised oil inside the oil pump housing.
Failure Symptom
Slow weep from the front of the engine, indistinguishable from front crank seal without proper inspection.
Field Impact
Access requires timing cover removal — do with timing belt job.
1.6  Valve Cover (Rocker) Gasket
FIRE RISK
Perimeter of rocker cover · top of head
What It Seals
Engine oil inside the rocker cover above the valve train.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep down the side of the head, visible as black streak. Smell of burning oil under load.
Field Impact
Drips onto exhaust manifold (600°C with turbo). Fire risk. Replace immediately — 20-minute job, cheap part. RTV bead acceptable as field fix if gasket unavailable.
1.7  Valve Cover Bolt Grommets
COSMETIC
Under each rocker cover bolt
What It Seals
Small rubber washers under each cover bolt. Part of the valve cover gasket kit.
Failure Symptom
Oil weeps from under individual bolt heads even with a new gasket.
Field Impact
Always replace with main gasket — do not reuse.
1.8  Oil Filler Cap O-Ring
COSMETIC
Inside the oil filler cap
What It Seals
Crankcase breather pressure at the fill point.
Failure Symptom
Oil mist escapes around the cap; smell of oil in the engine bay.
Field Impact
Replace the cap entirely — O-rings not sold separately.
1.9  Head Gasket
CRITICAL
Block deck to cylinder head · 12-bolt clamped
What It Seals
Three circuits: combustion (>1000 bar), coolant, oil.
Failure Symptom
White exhaust steam · bubbles in coolant reservoir · mayonnaise under oil cap. Any one sign: stop engine.
Field Impact
Complete strip and rebuild. Never field-fixable. The 1HZ only fails this when overheated — monitor temperature gauge constantly. See R4 §2.1 and R18 cooling section.
1.10  Oil Cooler Sandwich Plate O-Rings
MONITOR
Where oil cooler mounts to block
What It Seals
Engine oil flow between the block and the oil cooler heat exchanger.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at oil cooler mating face; can also cross-contaminate with coolant internally.
Field Impact
Replace O-rings at any sign of seep. If coolant appears in oil, cooler itself has failed internally — a bigger job.
1.11  Oil Pressure Sender Seal Washer
COSMETIC
Threaded into block near oil filter
What It Seals
Engine oil at the pressure sender threads.
Failure Symptom
Slow drip on the side of the block.
Field Impact
Crush washer — replace on removal. Carry a selection.
1.12  Dipstick Tube O-Ring
COSMETIC
Base of dipstick tube · into block
What It Seals
Crankcase at the dipstick tube entry point.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the base of the tube.
Field Impact
Trivial. Replace on removal.
1.13  Sump (Oil Pan) Gasket
MONITOR
Full perimeter · sump to block
What It Seals
Engine oil reservoir at the bottom of the block.
Failure Symptom
Oil drip at any point along the sump-to-block seam.
Field Impact
Drops require sump removal (R4 §1.2). Vulnerable to impact damage — bash plate protects it. Field patch: JB Weld on cleaned area.
1.14  Sump Drain Plug Washer
ROUTINE
Under drain plug
What It Seals
Engine oil at the drain point.
Failure Symptom
Drip from drain plug after oil change.
Field Impact
Crush washer. Replace at every oil change. Carry spares.
1.15  Rear Main Crankshaft Seal
MAJOR JOB
Rear of block · into bellhousing
What It Seals
Engine oil at the crankshaft rear where it exits the block into the bellhousing.
Failure Symptom
Oil drip from the bellhousing drain hole. Often confused with gearbox input shaft seal — both drip from the same hole.
Field Impact
Requires gearbox and flywheel removal. Monitor level and top up — you can drive for thousands of km with a slow rear main weep. Replace with next clutch job.
1.16  Breather Hose Grommets
COSMETIC
Rocker cover breather to intake
What It Seals
Crankcase blow-by gases routed to intake.
Failure Symptom
Oil mist around the breather connections; slight intake vacuum loss.
Field Impact
Cheap to replace. Check when changing air filter.
03 — §2 Engine · Coolant-Sealing Surfaces

Coolant Seals

The 1HZ head gasket only fails from overheating — so every seal in the coolant circuit is effectively head-gasket protection. Any coolant leak is a priority item, regardless of how small.

2.1  Water Pump Gasket
MONITOR
Water pump to block
What It Seals
Coolant between pump housing and block.
Failure Symptom
Coolant weep at the pump mounting face.
Field Impact
Replace pump + gasket together (R4 §4.1). Typically done at timing belt service because the pump is behind the timing cover on the 1HZ.
2.2  Water Pump Face Seal (Internal)
EARLY WARNING
Internal · signalled via weep hole
What It Seals
Coolant inside the pump body against the spinning impeller shaft.
Failure Symptom
Coolant drip from the engineered weep hole under the pump body. This is Toyota's designed early-warning system.
Field Impact
Plan pump replacement at first drip from weep hole. You have days to weeks, not hours. A squeal at startup = bearing wear imminent.
2.3  Thermostat Housing Gasket
MONITOR
Front of cylinder head
What It Seals
Coolant at the housing-to-head mating face.
Failure Symptom
Weep at the housing, often seen as a crusty white deposit on the casting.
Field Impact
Replace gasket at first weep — do with thermostat replacement.
2.4  Coolant Hose Clamps & Hose Ends
MONITOR
Upper/lower radiator · heater · bypass
What It Seals
Functionally the hose end + clamp is the seal between the hose and its metal union.
Failure Symptom
Coolant weep at hose ends. Swollen or cracked hose.
Field Impact
Tighten or replace clamp. Carry spare stainless worm-drive clamps and full hose set (R3 §7).
2.5  Core Plugs (Freeze Plugs)
CATASTROPHIC
Side and rear of block · 38mm + 48mm
What It Seals
Cast-in coolant passages at the machining access points.
Failure Symptom
Sudden gush of coolant from the side or rear of the block. Rust-through failure from old plain water use.
Field Impact
Total coolant loss within minutes. You carry 2× spares (R3 §7.1). Field replacement is possible but access can be awkward depending on which plug.
2.6  Coolant Temp Sender Seal Washer
COSMETIC
Threaded into thermostat housing
What It Seals
Coolant at the sender threads.
Failure Symptom
Slow weep around sender body.
Field Impact
Copper crush washer. Replace on removal.
2.7  Heater Hose Unions at Firewall
MONITOR
Firewall · cabin heater feed/return
What It Seals
Coolant flow to the cabin heater core.
Failure Symptom
Coolant drip inside cabin on the passenger footwell, or in the engine bay at firewall.
Field Impact
A cabin coolant smell means heater core or firewall union leak. You can bypass the heater core entirely in the field by joining the feed and return hoses with a short bypass pipe.
2.8  Radiator Tank Crimp Seals
KNOWN WEAKNESS
Plastic tank · alloy core · crimped seam
What It Seals
The top and bottom plastic tanks are crimped to the alloy core with a rubber gasket behind the crimp.
Failure Symptom
Coolant weeping along the tank-to-core seam, usually on the top tank first.
Field Impact
Radiator replacement is the only real fix. Stop-leak is a last resort for limp-home. Note: your replacement radiator has a quality warning in Service History — cross-reference before trip.
04 — §3 Fuel System · Injection Pump & Injectors

Fuel Seals

The 1HZ Denso inline injection pump is the second most common leak source on a high-km 1HZ, after the valve cover. Forum consensus from Denso specialists is that the side covers and rear cover O-rings are the first to fail, typically around 250–300k km. Your vehicle is now in that window.

Fuel system cleanliness is non-negotiable. A grain of dust in the IP or injectors causes expensive damage. Any work on this system must be done in a clean environment, and any parts must be new or freshly cleaned.

3.1  IP Front Drive Shaft Oil Seal
CRITICAL
Between IP body and timing case
What It Seals
Engine oil in the timing case on one side, fuel/pump oil on the other.
Failure Symptom
Oil leak behind the pump; or rising oil level (fuel entering sump); or milky oil.
Field Impact
Replace with front crank + cam seal at timing belt service.
3.2  IP Side Cover O-Rings (×2)
#1 FAILURE
Side of injection pump body
What It Seals
Diesel fuel inside the pump body at the side access covers.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep on the side of the pump. Fuel smell in engine bay. Drips on front diff housing visible underneath.
Field Impact
Denso specialists identify these as the most common 1HZ pump leak. Can sometimes be replaced in situ. Best: full pump reseal by Dup Diesel pre-departure.
3.3  IP Rear Cover / Governor Gasket
#2 FAILURE
Rear end of IP body
What It Seals
Fuel inside the pump's governor chamber.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep at the back of the pump.
Field Impact
Usually requires pump removal for proper reseal.
3.4  IP Top Cover Gasket
MONITOR
Top of pump · over governor mechanism
What It Seals
Governor mechanism chamber.
Failure Symptom
Fuel weep on top of pump; drips onto intake manifold.
Field Impact
Full reseal job.
3.5  Speed Sensor / Tacho Drive O-Ring
MONITOR
Top cover of pump
What It Seals
Small shaft penetration at top of pump.
Failure Symptom
Small fuel drip at sensor body.
Field Impact
Known frequent weeper per forum reports. Replace with full reseal.
3.6  Delivery Valve Holder Copper Washers (×6)
HIGH PRESSURE
Top of IP · high-pressure line exits
What It Seals
High-pressure fuel at each of the 6 line takeoffs from the pump.
Failure Symptom
Fuel weep at the base of the injector lines where they meet the pump. Can also be confused with a split injector pipe.
Field Impact
Copper crush washers. Replace on any disturbance. Carry spares.
3.7  ACSD (Auto Cold Start Device) Seals
BLANK OFF
Top of IP · coolant circuit connection
What It Seals
The wax-element cold-start device uses coolant circulation; can leak both fuel and coolant.
Failure Symptom
Mixed fuel/coolant weep near the ACSD mechanism.
Field Impact
Forum consensus: if ACSD fails, fit a blanking plate rather than rebuild. Does not affect hot running.
3.8  Fuel Cut Solenoid O-Ring
MONITOR
Top of IP
What It Seals
Solenoid body into the pump housing.
Failure Symptom
Fuel weep at solenoid threads.
Field Impact
Replace O-ring on removal.
3.9  Fuel Inlet Banjo Washers
ROUTINE
Low-pressure fuel inlet to IP
What It Seals
Low-pressure fuel supply at banjo bolt.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep at banjo bolt.
Field Impact
Copper crush washers — replace on any disturbance. Carry a selection.
3.10  Injector Seat Copper Washer (×6)
CRITICAL
Under each injector body · in head
What It Seals
Combustion gas + injector body seat against the head.
Failure Symptom
Black soot halo around one injector. Hot-starting trouble on that cylinder. Whistling noise under load.
Field Impact
Combustion blow-by erodes the washer seat on the head over time. Catch early — damaged seats require machining. Copper; always replace on any injector removal.
3.11  Injector Return Line O-Rings (×6)
CARRY
Top of each injector · return line banjo
What It Seals
Low-pressure fuel return from injector back to tank.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep on top of the head at each injector. Fuel smell in engine bay.
Field Impact
You already carry these per R3. Quick field fix — 5 minutes per injector.
3.12  Nozzle Cap Nut Internal Seal
WORKSHOP
Inside each injector
What It Seals
Internal injector pressure at the nozzle stack.
Failure Symptom
Hard starting · knock · rough idle · smoke. Identified by diesel compression tester.
Field Impact
Not field serviceable. Injector sent to Dup Diesel for test and recondition.
3.13  Fuel Filter Housing O-Rings
ROUTINE
Primary + secondary filter bowls
What It Seals
Diesel inside filter housings.
Failure Symptom
Fuel weep at filter base or where housing meets head.
Field Impact
Replace with every filter change. Secondary pre-filter (planned but not yet installed) will add another set.
3.14  Water-in-Fuel Sensor O-Ring
MONITOR
Base of primary filter bowl
What It Seals
Diesel at the sensor/drain screw.
Failure Symptom
Diesel drip under the filter after water drain cycle.
Field Impact
Replace with any drain-plug service.
3.15  Lift Pump Diaphragm & Cover Gasket
CARRY KIT
Fuel lift pump · mounted on block
What It Seals
Diaphragm isolates engine oil from low-pressure fuel; cover gasket seals the pump housing.
Failure Symptom
Fuel in oil sump (rising level, thin oil, diesel smell); or external fuel weep.
Field Impact
You carry a diaphragm kit per R3. 30-minute field fix.
3.16  Fuel Line Banjo Washers (various)
ROUTINE
Every fuel line union
What It Seals
Fuel at every banjo bolt connection in the system.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep at union.
Field Impact
Copper crush washers. Carry a varied selection.
3.17  Fuel Tank Sender O-Ring
MONITOR
Top of fuel tank
What It Seals
Tank access at the level sender.
Failure Symptom
Diesel weep on top of tank; fuel smell in cabin.
Field Impact
Tank drop to replace. Not urgent unless weeping heavily.
05 — §4 Turbocharger

Turbo Seals (SAC Low-Boost)

Your SAC turbo is the highest-temperature component on the vehicle. Its seals run at exhaust-gas temperatures up to 600°C with constant oil feed — a harsh environment. Turbo reconned Aug 2023 by SAC at 224,406 km; current service life ~80,600 km on the rebuild.

4.1  Turbo Oil Feed Line Banjo Washers
CRITICAL
Top of turbo body · from engine oil gallery
What It Seals
Pressurised engine oil feed to the turbo bearing.
Failure Symptom
Oil leak at the feed line fitting · smoke · oil drip onto exhaust manifold.
Field Impact
Fire risk on the manifold. Copper crush washers. Replace on any disturbance. Loss of oil feed kills turbo bearing in seconds.
4.2  Turbo Oil Drain Flange Gasket
MONITOR
Base of turbo · to block
What It Seals
Oil return from turbo back to the sump.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the turbo base · drips to bell housing area.
Field Impact
Metal gasket, usually simple flange replacement when turbo is off.
4.3  Turbo Compressor Shaft Seal
WEAR WARNING
Internal · compressor side
What It Seals
Engine oil from entering the compressed-air intake.
Failure Symptom
Oily film inside the intercooler piping. Blue smoke under load. Oil drips from intercooler hose joiners.
Field Impact
Indicates approaching turbo rebuild. Not a field repair. Check intercooler pipes regularly (R4 §7.5).
4.4  Turbo Turbine Shaft Seal
WEAR WARNING
Internal · exhaust side
What It Seals
Engine oil from entering the exhaust stream.
Failure Symptom
Blue smoke on overrun (when lifting off throttle). Smell of burning oil from exhaust.
Field Impact
Requires turbo rebuild. Back to SAC for reconditioning.
4.5  Exhaust Manifold Gasket
CARRY
Between manifold and head
What It Seals
Exhaust gas at each of the 6 ports.
Failure Symptom
Rhythmic tick at idle · loss of boost · tick disappears at revs.
Field Impact
You carry a spare (R3 §1.5). Field replacement 60–90 min.
4.6  Turbo-to-Downpipe Gasket
MONITOR
Exit side of turbo
What It Seals
Exhaust gas at the turbine outlet.
Failure Symptom
Exhaust leak noise at the turbo base · soot streaks.
Field Impact
Studs and gasket — not urgent but annoying. Done with any exhaust work.
4.7  Intercooler Hose Clamps & Silicone Joiners
BOOST LEAK
Turbo outlet to intake manifold
What It Seals
Compressed air between turbo and engine.
Failure Symptom
Loud pop or hiss under load · sudden power loss · black smoke · whistling noise.
Field Impact
Most common turbo fault. Retighten clamps — they vibrate loose. Soapy water test at idle. See R4 field fix "Turbo Boost Leak Diagnosis".
06 — §5 Gearbox R151F

Gearbox Seals

The R151F uses Castrol Transmax Manual 75W-90. Total capacity is small, so any leak drops the level fast. Gearbox seals are generally robust but age catches up with all of them at 300k km.

5.1  Front Input Shaft Seal
MAJOR JOB
Behind clutch release bearing · in bellhousing
What It Seals
Gearbox oil at the input shaft where it enters the clutch housing.
Failure Symptom
Oil drip from the bellhousing drain hole — same hole as rear main seal. Contaminates the clutch.
Field Impact
Requires gearbox removal. Replace with next clutch job. If dripping heavily, check clutch for slip — oil on friction plate causes slip.
5.2  Rear Output Shaft Seal
MONITOR
Rear of gearbox · at transfer case adapter
What It Seals
Gearbox oil at the output shaft feeding the transfer case.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the gearbox-to-transfer case joint.
Field Impact
Usually shared with transfer case input seal. Access requires transfer case removal.
5.3  Shift Tower Gasket
MONITOR
Top of gearbox · under gear lever boot
What It Seals
Oil at the shift tower base.
Failure Symptom
Oil smell in cabin · oil on the outside of the tower · wet rubber boot.
Field Impact
Cover removal and gasket replacement from the cabin side. Straightforward.
5.4  Side / Inspection Plate Gasket
MONITOR
Side of gearbox case
What It Seals
Oil at the access cover.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the side cover perimeter.
Field Impact
Paper gasket or RTV bead. Simple replacement.
5.5  Drain & Fill Plug Washers

Underside · side of gearbox

Oil at the drain and fill points.
Failure Symptom
Drip from plug after oil change.
Field Impact

5.6  Speedo Drive O-Ring
COSMETIC
Gearbox output · speedo cable entry
What It Seals
Oil at the speedo drive gear housing.
Failure Symptom
Small drip at speedo cable union.
Field Impact
Replace on removal.
5.7  Reverse Light Switch Washer
COSMETIC
Side of gearbox
What It Seals
Oil at reverse switch threads.

08 — §7 Front Axle

Front Axle Seals — the Big One

This is the single most seal-dense area on the vehicle. Each front corner has approximately 12 individual seals — inner and outer axle, knuckle ball, trunnions, Birfield, hub bearings, free-wheel hub, and the hub cap. Your Oct 2023 axle seal work and Nov 2023 hub set replacement at Matatiele were in this zone, and it's the area most likely to need attention again before departure.

You have prior history with the front diff. A pinion seal has been replaced at least once per project notes. Expect this to be a recurring maintenance item and plan for a full knuckle rebuild pre-departure if there is any sign of weep.

Per Corner (×2 sides)

KEY SEAL
Between axle tube and steering knuckle
What It Seals
Separates diff oil from Birfield grease at the inner knuckle.
Failure Symptom

Field Impact
Full front axle seal kit replacement. You carry this per R3 §5.1.
7.6  Knuckle Ball Gasket / Felt Wiper
COMMON WEEP
Around the chrome knuckle ball
What It Seals
Knuckle grease inside the chromed ball as it rotates for steering.
Failure Symptom
Grease on the swivel housing and back of the wheel. Classic symptom you already document in R3.
Field Impact
Half-day job per side to rebuild. Carry full axle seal kit.
7.7  Upper Trunnion (Kingpin) Seal
MONITOR
Top of steering knuckle
What It Seals
Grease inside the upper kingpin bearing.
Failure Symptom
Grease weep at top of knuckle · steering becomes stiff or notchy.
Field Impact
Part of full knuckle rebuild kit.
7.8  Lower Trunnion Seal
MONITOR
Bottom of steering knuckle
What It Seals
Grease inside the lower kingpin bearing.
Failure Symptom
Grease weep at bottom of knuckle.
Field Impact
Same as upper — part of full knuckle rebuild.
7.9  Trunnion Shims & Paper Gaskets
SHIM SET
Between trunnion cap and knuckle
What It Seals
Preload adjustment for the kingpin bearing; also seals the joint.
Failure Symptom
Weep at the trunnion cap face.
Field Impact
Shim adjustment required on reassembly for correct preload. Critical for steering feel.
7.10  Knuckle Inspection Plate Gasket
COSMETIC
Back of knuckle
What It Seals
Access cover for inspecting the Birfield.
Failure Symptom
Grease weep at the cover perimeter.
Field Impact
Paper gasket or RTV. Trivial.
7.11  Birfield Inner Seal
CRITICAL GREASE
Inner end of Birfield cup
What It Seals
Moly grease inside the Birfield cup.
Failure Symptom
Clicking or clunking on full lock in 4WD — grease lost, balls running dry.
Field Impact
Birfield destruction if not caught. Repack with Toyota moly grease only — never general bearing grease (R4 §11.5). Full knuckle rebuild.
7.12  Hub Inner Grease Seal
MONITOR
Inside wheel hub · behind inner bearing
What It Seals
Wheel bearing grease; prevents diff oil / knuckle grease from reaching the brakes.
Failure Symptom
Grease or oil on front brake rotors · brake squeal or grab.
Field Impact
Brake contamination is unsafe. Replace with wheel bearing service. Repack bearings (R3 §5.1 field note).
7.13  Hub-to-Spindle Outer Felt
WEATHER SEAL
Outer face of hub
What It Seals
Protects the outer bearing from dust and water ingress.
Failure Symptom
Grit in bearing · bearing noise on wheel rotation.
Field Impact
Always replace at bearing service.
7.14  Free-Wheel Hub Body O-Ring
WATER ENTRY
Where auto-hub meets the spindle
What It Seals
Water and dust from entering the hub engagement mechanism.
Failure Symptom
Auto-hub fails to engage · clicking or no drive to front wheel in 4WD · water inside hub after crossing.
Field Impact
Your Nov 2023 hub replacement addressed this. Expected wear item. Use manual LOCK before crossings — don't trust auto mechanism when it matters.
7.15  Free-Wheel Hub Cap Gasket
WEATHER SEAL
Outermost end cap on wheel centre
What It Seals
Seals the free-wheel hub cap to the hub body.
Failure Symptom
Water ingress after crossings · grease flung out during wheel rotation.
Field Impact
Inspect at any wheel removal. Cheap to replace.
09 — §8 Rear Axle

Rear Axle Seals

Full-floating rear axle — simpler than the front by a large margin. The advantage: fewer seals, and a broken rear axle shaft can be removed in 10 minutes without dropping the wheel (R4 §11.8).

8.1  Rear Pinion Nose Seal
MONITOR
Front of rear diff · propshaft flange
What It Seals
Rear diff oil at the pinion shaft.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the rear pinion flange.
Field Impact
Standard replacement. You can drive for thousands of km with a slow weep if you top up.
8.2  Rear Axle Shaft Hub Flange O-Ring
MONITOR
Under 6-bolt flange at each rear hub
What It Seals
Diff oil at the shaft flange where it bolts to the hub.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at the 6-bolt flange on the hub face.
Field Impact
Simple fix — remove shaft, replace O-ring, refit.
8.3  Rear Hub Wheel Bearing Grease Seal (×2)
BRAKE RISK
Inside each rear hub
What It Seals
Wheel bearing grease; isolates from diff oil and brakes.
Failure Symptom
Oil or grease on rear brake drums · brake contamination · brake grab or squeal.
Field Impact
Brake safety risk. Repack bearings at service intervals. Replace seals after any water crossing (R3 §5.4).
8.4  Rear Diff Breather
EXTEND
Top of diff housing · hose to snorkel
What It Seals
Pressure equalisation; water keep-out on submersion.
Failure Symptom
Milky oil after crossing.
Field Impact
Extend before every trip. Same principle as front diff.
8.5  Drain / Fill Plug Washers
ROUTINE
Rear diff housing
What It Seals
Diff oil at service points.
Failure Symptom
Drip from plug.
Field Impact
Crush washers. Stock spares.
8.6  Rear Diff Inspection Cover Gasket
MONITOR
Back of diff housing (if applicable)
What It Seals
Access cover for diff internals.
Failure Symptom
Oil weep at cover perimeter.
Field Impact
Paper gasket or RTV. Done with any diff service.
10 — §9 Brakes & Clutch Hydraulics

Brake & Clutch Seals

Critical safety-item seals. The brake and clutch hydraulic systems share the same fluid reservoir on this vehicle — a clutch leak shows up as dropping brake fluid level, so any unexplained fluid drop means check both circuits.

Shared reservoir warning. A failing clutch slave cylinder can pull fluid from the brake circuit. If brake fluid drops and brakes feel normal, check the clutch slave before assuming a brake leak.

9.1  Brake Master Cylinder Internal Seals
CRITICAL
Engine bay · firewall
What It Seals
Brake fluid pressure inside the master cylinder bores.
Failure Symptom
Brake pedal slowly drops to the floor under sustained pressure. Often no external leak. Potentially total brake loss.
Field Impact
Master replacement — do not try to rebuild in the field. Carry complete spare unit if possible.
9.2  Clutch Master Cylinder Seals
CRITICAL
Engine bay · firewall · shares reservoir
What It Seals
Clutch hydraulic pressure.
Failure Symptom
Soft clutch pedal · pedal to the floor · brake fluid dropping in reservoir with no brake leak.
Field Impact
Loss of clutch. You can shift without clutch in emergency (match revs) — see R13 Field Fixes.
9.3  Clutch Slave Cylinder Seal & Dust Boot
COMMON FAILURE
On bellhousing · passenger side
What It Seals
Clutch hydraulic pressure at the slave piston.
Failure Symptom
Most common clutch hydraulic failure. Fluid visible around bellhousing · dropping reservoir · spongy pedal.
Field Impact
Carry a complete spare slave cylinder, not just fluid. 30-minute field replacement (R13 Field Fixes). You already carry a bleed kit + DOT 4 (R3 §5.5).
9.4  Front Brake Caliper Piston Seals (×2)
SAFETY
Behind each front caliper piston
What It Seals
Brake fluid inside each caliper bore.
Failure Symptom
Fluid weep behind pad · squealing brake · dragging brake · dropping reservoir level.
Field Impact
Caliper rebuild kit. Replace boot and square-section seal. Don't drive with a contaminated disc.
9.5  Rear Wheel Cylinder Cup Seals (×2)
SAFETY
Inside each rear drum
What It Seals
Brake fluid inside each rear wheel cylinder.
Failure Symptom
Brake fluid soaked into drum lining · rear brake imbalance · dropping reservoir.
Field Impact
Replace wheel cylinder rather than rebuild in field. Clean or replace shoes if contaminated.
9.6  Brake Flex Hose Ends
AGE FAIL
Each wheel corner · chassis to caliper
What It Seals
Crimped end of flex hose to banjo fitting.
Failure Symptom
Bulging hose under pressure · weep at crimp · brake dragging (internal delamination).
Field Impact
Replace all four flex hoses pre-departure if age unknown. Stainless braided upgrade worth considering.
9.7  Brake Line Flare Washers
ROUTINE
Hard line unions
What It Seals
Brake fluid at some hard-line unions (copper washers where used).
Failure Symptom
Weep at union.
Field Impact
Replace on any line disturbance.
9.8  Proportioning Valve Seals
MONITOR
Load-sensing valve on rear axle
What It Seals
Brake fluid in the load-sensing valve body.
Failure Symptom
Weep at the valve body · rear brake imbalance.
Field Impact
Replace valve assembly — not usually field-rebuildable.
9.9  Vacuum Brake Booster Seal
PEDAL HARDNESS
Between booster and master cylinder
What It Seals
Vacuum inside the brake booster chamber.
Failure Symptom
Hard brake pedal · high effort required · engine idle change when braking.
Field Impact
Booster replacement. Brakes still work without vacuum assist, just very heavy.

What It Seals
Power steering fluid at the input shaft.
Failure Symptom

15 — §14 Pre-Departure Seal Inspection Protocol

Pre-Trip Seal Inspection — 20 Points

A systematic walk-around to catch weeps before they become trip-enders. Do this at every pre-expedition check and at every major service. Designed to print as a standalone A4 page for the workshop.

Engine Bay (clean first, then inspect)

01
Valve cover perimeter — any oil weep down the sides of the head. Check all cover bolts finger-tight. Sniff for burning oil.
02
Front of engine / timing cover — oil film on pulley, radiator, crossmember. Any drip = front crank/cam/IP drive seal. Fix before next timing belt.
03
Water pump weep hole — any coolant drip from the underside of the pump body = internal seal failing. Plan replacement.
04
Injection pump all sides — clean pump, run engine, torch inspection. Particular attention to side covers, rear cover, delivery valves.
05
Turbo area — oil feed banjo, drain flange, compressor inlet. Pull an intercooler hose end to check for oil film inside = compressor seal wear.
06
All coolant hoses — squeeze each cold. Soft/swollen = replace. Check clamps for corrosion.
07
Power steering pump and box — check fluid colour on pump nose, box input and sector shafts. ATF Dexron II is reddish — distinguishes from engine oil.
08
Brake & clutch reservoir — fluid level at MAX? Any unexplained drop = master/slave/caliper/wheel cylinder leak.

Underside (on ramps or pit)

09
Sump & bellhousing drain hole — any drip? Rear main seal or gearbox input seal. Note fluid type.
10
Gearbox case and plugs — drain and fill plugs torqued? Side covers dry? Shift tower base dry?
11
Transfer case front output — most common weep point. Check directly below front output flange for oil drip.
12
Transfer case shift rods — three shift rod entries on top. Any oil dampness?
13
Front diff pinion seal — check directly behind the front propshaft flange. Your known weep area — inspect carefully.
14
Rear diff pinion seal — same check at rear propshaft flange.
15
Front and rear diff breathers — both extended to snorkel height? Hoses intact? No kinks? Non-negotiable before any water.
16
Front knuckles — both sides — grease or oil on swivel housing? Grease on back of wheel rim? Full knuckle rebuild required if present.
17
Free-wheel hub end caps — check both front hubs for water staining or grease weeping. Test manual LOCK operation.

Wheel Off (one corner at a time)

18
Front brake rotors — clean and dry? Any grease or oil contamination from hub seal = do not depart until fixed.
19
Rear brake drum inner face — any fluid staining? Wheel cylinder or hub seal leak — safety critical.
20
Shock absorbers F & R — oil streaks down the body of any shock = seal failing, replace as a set.

Workflow tip. Pressure-wash the underside and engine bay thoroughly a day before inspection, then drive a short distance. Any fresh oil on a clean surface will be visible and traceable to its source. Inspecting a filthy vehicle is useless.
