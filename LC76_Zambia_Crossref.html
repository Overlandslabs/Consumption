EV Environmental Issues — For Profile Context Background facts to weave into country narrative
IssueKey fact
Poaching / snaringWire snare epidemic is the most widespread threat to Zambia's wildlife; kills indiscriminately — eland, wild dog, lion all lost; SLCS runs community anti-poaching with local monitors in Luangwa Valley
Elephant recoveryZambia's elephant population declined from ~200,000 (1970s) to under 20,000 (1990s) through poaching; partial recovery under improved management but snaring remains a persistent pressure
CBNRM / GMAsGame Management Areas buffer national parks; wildlife revenue meant to flow to community chieftaincy under CBNRM model; distribution is often contested — choosing camps that demonstrably invest in community and anti-poaching is the most effective traveller lever
Kafue Flats hydrologyItezhi-Tezhi Dam (1977) regulates Kafue River flow; altered seasonal flood pulse has reduced Kafue lechwe population from ~150,000 to ~50,000 — a well-documented conservation cost of the dam
Bat migration — conservationKasanka's bat migration (10M+ straw-coloured fruit bats) is one of Africa's great wildlife events; trust management has protected the roost; hunting pressure on bats outside the park remains a threat
Teak logging — Caprivi / western ZambiaZambezi teak (Baikiaea plurijuga) has been heavily logged for furniture timber in western Zambia; slow-growing species — regeneration takes centuries; note in any profile section covering western Zambia / Liuwa Plain
