Methodology & notes
■ How the model calculates
Baseline Your recorded real-world km/L is the anchor. All adjustments scale from it multiplicatively.
Terrain multiplier Divides baseline by a surface resistance factor (×1.0 highway → ×1.8 technical).
Wind multiplier Headwind increases aerodynamic drag; tailwind gives a small benefit (×0.95–×1.22).
Hilliness multiplier Net gravity load on climbs — descents don’t recover fuel efficiently (×1.0–×1.20).
Load penalty Applied only above your recording load. Extra kg above that point reduces km/L.
Tyre pressure Penalty relative to your recording pressure. Terrain buttons suggest a sensible value; you can override. Low pressure increases rolling resistance, high pressure gives a small benefit. The recording pressure in setup should match the conditions under which your baseline km/L was measured — change it only if your logged average was recorded at a non-standard pressure.
Altitude Thinner air reduces combustion density. Turbos partially compensate; intercoolers more so. Coefficient set by engine type.
Temperature Hot air above 25°C reduces combustion density (0.2%/°C above baseline).
Stacking All factors are multiplicative — mountain pass + deep sand + strong headwind is worst-case and they compound, not add.
■ General notes
Injection pump timing Small adjustments make a measurable difference to economy on mechanical diesel engines. Requires a specialist — not a DIY task.
RPM discipline Most diesels make torque in a narrow band. Avoid lugging at low speed — change down to keep the engine in its efficient range rather than fighting it in top gear.
Speed and aerodynamics Fuel cost rises steeply with speed on high-frontal-area vehicles. The difference between 100 km/h and 120 km/h is rarely worth it on fuel or tyres.
Tyre condition Worn or under-inflated tyres both increase rolling resistance beyond what the pressure slider models. Check and adjust before long legs.
Cold starts and short runs The baseline assumes a warm engine at operating temperature. Cold starts and urban stop-start driving will underperform the estimate.
Accuracy These are estimates based on your recorded average with modelled adjustments. Actual consumption varies with driver technique, wind variation, terrain quality, and mechanical condition.
Always carry more than the calculation suggests. Verify your multipliers against your own field logs over time and adjust your baseline accordingly.
