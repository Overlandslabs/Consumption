WEIGHT & PAYLOAD

⚠ GVM limit: 2,850 kg — never exceed

Exceeding GVM compromises braking, handling, suspension and insurance. Weigh at a public weighbridge before every major trip.

Payload Budget

All kerb weight figures are estimates — accessory weights from published specs where available, otherwise estimated. Weigh the vehicle at a public weighbridge to establish true kerb weight before expedition departure.

GVM (Gross Vehicle Mass) is the maximum legal total weight — vehicle, fuel, passengers, cargo, and all accessories combined. Kerb weight is the vehicle ready to drive with no passengers or cargo. The difference is your payload.

Section 5

LOADING PRINCIPLES

⚠ Projectile risk

A 5 kg item at 60 km/h exerts 150+ kg force. Cargo barrier is life-saving equipment.

Loading Hierarchy

  1. Heavy low, between axles: Fridge, water, ammo, tools, fluids
  2. Medium mid-height, secured: Containers, drawers, bags
  3. Light on top / roof: Bedding, tent, chairs. ≤60 kg dynamic
  4. Frequent-access at openings: Fridge side, camera seat-delete, medical under seat, recovery window box
  5. Emergency — zero digging: First aid, fire extinguisher, hammer, torch

Packing Discipline

  • Standardise containers — uniform size, tessellate, eliminate shifting
  • Label everything — especially identical ammo boxes
  • One home for every item — no spot = question if it comes
  • Reverse order of use: first-needed = last-loaded or always accessible
  • Bag discipline: dry bags, compression sacks, all roof bedding waterproofed
  • "Airbnb repack": keep pack system intact during breaks
  • Daily-driver mode: seat-delete platform works without full gear
