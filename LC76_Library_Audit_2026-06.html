1 · Scope & Method

What was audited, against what benchmark, and on what evidence.

Objective. Assess the LC76 Overland Reference Library against the standard appropriate to its purpose — self-reliant, long-duration, remote overland travel in Southern & East Africa — and identify coverage gaps, currency risks, redundancy, data-integrity issues and governance defects, with prioritised remediation.

Benchmark. The yardstick is best practice for a privately-operated, single-vehicle expedition that must be largely self-sufficient for mechanical repair, medical response, water, fuel, navigation and security across multiple borders over roughly two years — not casual weekend overlanding.

Evidence base (verified live this session). Library inventory taken from the live Register and index.html on GitHub (dated 14 Jun 2026), cross-read against Facts.txt. Thirteen candidate documents were fetched from raw.githubusercontent.com and probed for embedded coverage of the domains flagged as potential gaps — using synonym-aware text probes followed by passage-level reading, not keyword counts. Findings affected by that verification are marked [verified].
Deferred (not yet performed). A line-by-line technical-accuracy review of all 45 documents against external sources, and currency re-verification of fast-moving content (comms tech, border/fee/fuel data), are Phase-2 work. This report is a structural, coverage and governance audit, plus the verification of embedded coverage that was authorised as the first step.

2 · Overall Verdict

Headline assessment and the correction trail from the first-pass review.

For a single-vehicle private expedition the library sits well above typical standard. Depth on the 1HZ powertrain, electrical/battery systems, tyres, recovery and the country profiles is strong, and the governance spine — a single-source Facts.txt, an authoritative Register, and an outstanding-items tracker — is genuinely audit-grade and rare in this field.

The weaknesses are not in depth but in breadth and balance. A small number of safety-relevant domains that best practice treats as core lack a consolidated home, while two domains (electrical, philosophy) each carry six and two documents respectively. The single most material issue is not a document at all — it is the unreconciled kerb/payload figure that quietly undermines load and tyre-pressure planning downstream.

Correction trail. The first-pass review flagged brakes, first-aid, fire, food and money as apparent gaps. Verified reading reclassified most of these as covered: brakes are competently treated across R23/R25/R27/R28/R3; first-aid and snakebite are covered in R10 and R17; vehicle-fire response is in R10; food safety and emergency rationing are in R17/R10/R11. This is why the embedded-coverage check was run before any conclusion — three would-be "gaps" did not survive contact with the documents.

4 · Findings Register

Fourteen findings, grouped by category. Eight now resolved (F1–F4, F7, F11, F12, F14). The one High (F10, weighbridge) remains open; the rest are timing- or data-bound.

A · Coverage
F1Security, checkpoints & theft prevention — no consolidated protocol
RESOLVED · R35
ObservationFacets exist — R15 covers checkpoint frequency, on-the-spot fines and document discipline; R29 covers theft and civil-unrest insurance exclusions; R10 carries a "Security / Isolation" card and emergency numbers — but there is no single field reference for conduct at a corrupt roadblock (de-escalation, requesting a written ticket, photocopies vs originals), urban crime avoidance, or camp/vehicle theft prevention.
Why it mattersFor Southern/East Africa this is a defining overland competence; it is the clearest real gap relative to the region.
ActionDraft a consolidated Security & Checkpoints field guide; harvest the existing R15/R29/R10 fragments rather than start blank.
F2Dangerous-game encounter & camp safety — thin
RESOLVED · R36
ObservationSnakebite is well covered (R10 §5 protocols; R17 "any bite → hospital"). Big-game behaviour — elephant, hippo, lion, buffalo, crocodile encounters and camp conduct in unfenced areas — appears only as scattered notes inside country profiles, with no consolidated reference.
Why it mattersTwo years through big-game country with ground tents and no vehicle sleeping raises the stakes on camp-selection and encounter behaviour.
ActionConsolidate a Dangerous Wildlife & Camp Safety reference; cross-ref R28 (night/animals) and the profiles.
F3Brakes — covered but not consolidated; no bleed procedure; monitor not echoed [verified]
RESOLVED · R34
ObservationBrakes are not a gap. R23 schedules pad/rotor/drum inspection (front pad min 4 mm; "carry rear brake shoe set as spares") and full fluid change + bleed; R25 inspects brake-disc weep and hose chafing; R27 carries brake-line field doctrine (a brake-line leak is a STOP condition — no hose-clamp or JB-Weld bodge); R28 covers braking technique and fade; R3 covers the shared clutch/brake hydraulic circuit. What is genuinely thin: a step-by-step bleed procedure, and the rear-hub-seal → brake-shoe-contamination monitor recorded in Facts §3 is not echoed anywhere a user would look for brakes.
Why it mattersThe material is good but distributed; a single consolidated brake reference closes the procedure gap and surfaces an active monitor in the right place. (Owner-nominated lead document if drafting proceeds.)
ActionConsolidated Brake reference: gather existing content + add bleed sequence (all four corners), a brake-fault diagnosis table, and the rear hub-seal contamination link.
F4Fire safety — vehicle-fire response only [verified]
RESOLVED · R35
ObservationR10 §3 covers engine/vehicle fire response; R15 notes the legal extinguisher requirement; R11 places the extinguisher in zero-dig access; R27 flags flammable-solvent discipline. Thin on extinguisher type/rating/placement, fuel-handling/refuelling discipline, and campfire/bush-fire discipline (exhaust on dry grass).
Why it mattersLow-probability, high-consequence; cheap to close.
ActionFold a short Fire & Fuel-Handling section into the Security guide (F1) or expand R10 — standalone not required.
F5Provisioning & forex — strategy layer only [verified]
LOW
ObservationFood safety (R17), load (R11), resupply cadence (R12) and emergency rationing (R10) are all covered; border cash discipline is in R15 and profiles. Absent only as a strategic layer: long-leg provisioning planning and a consolidated multi-country cash/forex/ATM/budget reference.
Why it mattersConvenience, not safety. Defer unless wanted.
ActionOptional; revisit closer to departure when the route firms up.
B · Currency & Staleness
F6Country profiles will age out before departure
MED
ObservationAll six profiles are dated Apr–May 2026. Fuel prices, park/visa fees, booking processes and border rules carry a ~6–12 month shelf life; by a 2028 departure every profile needs a full re-verification. No refresh cadence is currently defined.
Why it mattersStale fees/rules drive bad budgeting and border surprises.
ActionAdopt a stated cadence — e.g. a full T-minus-6-months re-verify of every profile, with a "verified-as-at" date stamped in each.
F7Comms & navigation (R16) currency
RESOLVED · R16
ObservationSatellite comms is the fastest-moving domain. R16 was read and verified against the 2026 picture (Jun 2026): a Starlink section was added (route legal status, the ~2-month Roam cap, an LC76 power note cross-ref R26), phone-based satellite flagged as a tertiary backup, the IsatPhone 2 confirmed still current, and GEOS — Garmin Response corrected. Empirical claims stamped 2026 and flagged “verify” where ambiguous — worth a re-check nearer departure.
Why it mattersLife-safety reference; ranks above the profiles for re-check.
ActionPhase-2: read R16, verify against current sources, update as needed.
F8Geographic coverage vs stated route
MED
Observation"Southern and East Africa" but no profiles for the obvious through-routes and termini — Zimbabwe, Mozambique, Malawi, Kenya (and likely Uganda/Rwanda). Reasonable as a roadmap item; named here so it does not surprise in 2027.
Why it mattersThe link countries are where a two-year route actually joins up.
ActionSequence profile builds against the firming route; no action needed now beyond acknowledging the roadmap.
C · Redundancy & Maintenance Burden
F9Electrical domain is six documents deep with interim layering
MED
ObservationR5, R6, R7, R26, R32, R33 (plus the calculator), several explicitly "interim, superseded by R26 at LiFePO4 install". Overlap and supersession create contradiction and maintenance risk.
Why it mattersSix documents must stay mutually consistent through the LiFePO4 transition.
ActionPlan the consolidation now; execute at the ~2027 LiFePO4 install, collapsing the interim set into the full R26. R27’s electrical fault cards also describe the not-yet-fitted LiFePO4 system (BMV-712, Orion-Tr, 200 Ah bank) — correct them in the same pass.
D · Data Integrity
F10Kerb / payload unreconciled — gates load & pressure planning
HIGH
ObservationTwo figures circulate (~690 kg vs ~551 kg payload; ~2,160 vs ~2,299 kg kerb) — a ~140 kg spread, material against a 2,850 kg GVM. It propagates into R11 load maths and tyre-pressure baselines.
Why it mattersThe single most valuable owner action in the library; everything load- and pressure-related inherits this uncertainty.
ActionWeighbridge the fully-loaded vehicle (per-axle + GCM), reconcile to one figure, flow into R11 and the tyre-pressure baseline — ideally before the LiFePO4 install adds weight.

5 · What's Working

Recorded so remediation respects, rather than disturbs, what is already strong.

Governance spine
Single-source Facts.txt, an authoritative Register, a curl-before-edit discipline and an outstanding-items tracker. Rare and genuinely audit-grade.
Powertrain depth
1HZ engine, turbo, fuel system, drivetrain and troubleshooting are thorough and interlinked — the core of self-reliant travel.
Electrical knowledge
Six documents plus a power-budget calculator. Heavy for maintenance, but the underlying coverage is comprehensive.
Field calculators
Fuel range, trip fuel, fuel-log, water, power and tyre-pressure tools turn reference data into decisions in the field.
Profile granularity
Booking matrices, fee tiers, fuel prices and phrasebooks per country — far beyond typical trip notes (currency caveat aside).
Outstanding-items tracking
Facts §3 surfaces overdue valve clearances, water pump, thermostat and cooling flush — the library flags its own vehicle risks well.
