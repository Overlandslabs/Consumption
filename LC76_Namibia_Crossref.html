NWR booking system As of April 2026, the NWR online booking system has been intermittently unavailable. Book by email (reservations@nwr.com.na) and confirm via phone. Payment by EFT or credit card via email. Confirm system status before planning. Off-season (May–Jun, Sep–Oct) availability is good with 2–3 months lead time.
Communal conservancy fees — NAD cash mandatory Kaokoland communal conservancy camps (Marienfluss, Sanitatas, Puros, Sesfontein conservancies) accept walk-in arrivals only — no advance booking. Fee is paid to community representative or camp custodian on arrival. Carry sufficient NAD cash for the full Kaokoland circuit before leaving Opuwo. These fees directly fund community anti-poaching patrols.

Hoarusib riverbed flash-flood warning — mandatory with all Hoarusib route publications Any rain in the Kaokoland catchment (even unseen storms 100 km upstream) can send a wall of water down the Hoarusib in minutes. Check full-catchment weather before entering, not just local conditions. If any clouds visible over Kaokoland highlands, do not enter the riverbed. This warning must accompany any Hoarusib route information in any public-facing document.
EV Environmental Issues — For Profile Context Background facts for country narrative
IssueKey fact
Herero and Nama genocide1904–1908 German campaign killed estimated 65,000–80,000 Herero (~80% of population) and 10,000 Nama; Germany formally acknowledged and apologised 2021; reparations (€1.1bn development funding) contested by affected communities as inadequate; this history is directly relevant to Kaokoland travel context and community relationships
CBNRM successNamibia's communal conservancy model (86+ conservancies, 167,000 km²) is the world's most successful CBNRM programme; wildlife populations have recovered significantly since 1996 — lion, elephant, black rhino all increasing in conservancy areas; model depends on communities receiving genuine financial benefit from tourism
Rhino poaching pressure90+ rhinos poached 2012–2019 driven by Asian horn markets; active government and SRT anti-poaching response; community tracker network is front-line early warning; do not publish rhino GPS sightings publicly
Desert vegetation recoveryVehicle tracks in desert veld persist for decades — compaction alters drainage and inhibits germination; off-road driving in Kaokoland and Namib causes multi-generational damage; established tracks only
Caprivi teak loggingZambezi teak heavily logged for furniture timber in western Zambia and historically in Caprivi; species takes centuries to regenerate; note as ongoing threat in Caprivi conservation section
Bush War legacySouth African military operated extensively in the Caprivi during the Bush War (1966–1989); some communities in the Caprivi carry this history in memory; sensitivity warranted in interactions — this is not abstract history
