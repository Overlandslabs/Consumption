Scope of this profile Profile 007 covers southern Mozambique with a coastal focus, extended north to Beira / Sofala — Maputo, Gaza and Inhambane provinces (the classic self-drive belt from Ponta do Ouro to the Bazaruto coast) plus the Sofala approach to Beira. The northbound leg to Malawi (inland via the Tete corridor, central Mozambique) is deliberately out of scope here and is carried as a forward cross-reference — see the callout in §18 and the Malawi profile (008 / EX-5) and the route workshop (EX-9).
01 Entry & Visas SA passport · SADC visa-free · ETA in flux

For a South African passport holder, Mozambique is visa-free for tourism under the SADC bilateral arrangement — the standard grant is 30 days (some sources cite up to 90; the reliable working figure for a land crossing is 30 days, extendable in-country at a provincial migration office). This is one reason Mozambique is the natural first leg: minimal paperwork friction on the personal-entry side.

2026 change — verify before every crossing Mozambique launched a new digital entry platform (evisa.gov.mz) on 11 Feb 2026 and is phasing in an Electronic Travel Authorisation (ETA) plus a small entry tax (reported around 650 MZN / ~USD 10) that, in principle, applies even to visa-exempt travellers. Enforcement in early 2026 was inconsistent (land arrivals from SA have largely continued visa-free), but this is exactly the kind of rule that hardens without notice. Check evisa.gov.mz within a week of travel and complete any ETA at least 48 h ahead. If the expedition is split (a licence-break return to RSA and re-entry), plan for the multi-entry implications — a single-entry authorisation will not cover re-entry.

02 Border Crossings Lebombo main · Kosi Bay coastal · Giriyondo park

Three crossings serve southern Mozambique from South Africa. Lebombo / Ressano Garcia is the workhorse — busy, truck-heavy, but tarred all the way and 90-odd km from Maputo. Kosi Bay / Ponta do Ouro is the coastal option into the far south (sand roads, 4×4 needed). Giriyondo links Kruger to Limpopo National Park inside the Great Limpopo TFCA (seasonal hours, 4×4, park fees both sides).

04 Best Season & Timing Dry May–Oct · cyclones Nov–Apr

Southern Mozambique runs on two seasons. The dry, cooler winter (May–October) is the driving season: firmer sand tracks, clearer coastal water, fewer mosquitoes, and it dovetails with a Karoo-style RSA departure. The wet summer (November–April) is hot and humid and is cyclone season — the Mozambique Channel regularly spins up systems that flood the coastal plain and cut the EN1 (Cyclone Gezani, Feb 2026, is the recent example).

If a licence-break return lands you re-entering in mid-summer, treat cyclone tracking as a daily task and keep the coastal EN1 escape-north/south options in mind.
05 Fuel 50 ppm nationwide · dear · can be scarce

Mozambique moved its whole market to 50 ppm low-sulphur diesel in 2017, so the grade is fine for the 1HZ (which is in any case tolerant of higher sulphur — one of the reasons this engine was chosen). The live issues are price, supply reliability, and on-the-ground quality in smaller towns, not the sulphur spec.

Reliable fill points on the coastal spine: Maputo/Matola, Macia, Xai-Xai, Quissico, Maxixe, Inhambane, Massinga, Vilanculos and Inhassoro all have stations. Prefer the busy branded forecourts (Galp, Puma, Total, Engen) in the larger towns; be wary of low-volume rural pumps where old or watered stock sits. Use the water-separating pre-filter, drain it, and treat any suspect fill with caution — the 1HZ will run on poor diesel but the injection system (freshly overhauled Jun 2026) is worth protecting.

Ponta do Ouro fuel trap Fuel is frequently unavailable at Ponta do Ouro. If entering via Kosi Bay, cross with a full main tank and both cells full; don't rely on buying diesel until Maputo or a main-road town.

06 Parts & Serviceability 1HZ · tyres · Victron · fuel quality

This section is the expedition-readiness view: what can be sourced or serviced in-country, and what must be carried or deferred to South Africa. It feeds the R3 consumables ledger (EX-8). The short version for Mozambique: good general Toyota presence and strong 70-Series familiarity, but treat South Africa — one border away — as the real support base for anything specialist.

1HZ / Toyota parts & workshopsCFAO Mobility network

Toyota is represented by CFAO Mobility (Toyota Moçambique), with sales-and-service branches in Maputo (Toyota Gallery, Av. 25 de Setembro), Matola (parts shop + workshop), Beira (Entreposto Auto), and an expedition-oriented agent, VES — Vehicle Expedition Services, in Vilanculos (handy on the coastal route). The 70-Series is a regional workhorse, so mechanical familiarity and common consumables are widely available; genuine 1HZ-specific injection parts (VE-pump internals, injectors, nozzles) are not reliably stocked — carry those per R3.

Tyres — 265/70R16 (and planned 265/75R16)buy quality in RSA

265/70R16 is a common fitment (Hilux/Fortuner/Land Cruiser), so tyre fitment and basic stock exist in Maputo and Beira. But do not expect a matched Maxxis AT811 or a quality all-terrain in your size on demand — an emergency buy is likely to be whatever brand is on the shelf. The disciplined posture: enter on good rubber bought in RSA, carry a sound full-size spare, and carry the plug/repair kit + compressor. The future 265/75R16 upgrade should likewise be fitted in South Africa, not sourced on the road.

Victron / LiFePO4 supportRSA is the base

Victron components are sold and installed by solar-trade dealers in Maputo (e.g. SwissSolar, SOLMOZ and other off-grid installers) and carry Victron's global warranty, so a replacement MPPT, SmartShunt or fuse can in principle be obtained in the capital. There is, however, no 4×4-specific Victron fitment expertise on the road, and the system was installed near home by Manjaro Industries. Practical posture: the LiFePO4 house system is largely self-monitoring via VictronConnect Bluetooth; carry spare fuses and a VE.Direct cable, know the R26/R7 fault trees, and treat South Africa as the repair base. Because Mozambique is the first, closest country north of home, a serious electrical fault is often best resolved by returning to RSA or having parts shipped.

Fuel quality & 50 ppmsee also §05

50 ppm nationwide (2017); the 1HZ is untroubled by the grade. Water and particulate contamination in low-volume rural pumps is the real serviceability concern — run the pre-filter/water separator, drain it regularly, and favour high-turnover branded stations. Halve the effective oil-change confidence if you're forced onto suspect stock, and log every fill for the R3 ledger and post-trip review (R23).

Serviceability verdict — southern Mozambique. Green for consumables and general Toyota help; amber for anything 1HZ-specific, suspension/driveline, tyres-by-brand, or Victron. Net: self-sufficient to R3 spec, with RSA as a one-border fallback. This is the easiest country on the route to recover from — use it to shake down the spares list before the harder legs north.

07 Roads EN1 spine · coastal sand · potholes

The EN1 is the national artery running north up the coastal plain — mostly tar, but with pothole stretches, patchy edges and long gaps between towns. The EN4 from Ressano Garcia to Maputo is a good toll road. Off the EN1, the coastal access tracks to the beaches (Tofo, Barra, Vilanculos backroads, Ponta) are deep sand — deflate and engage 4×4.

Overtake only with clear sight; heavy truck traffic on the EN1 and EN4. Livestock and pedestrians on the road edge at all hours. No night driving.

08 Security South stable · EN1/Sofala corridor · protests

The security picture is two countries in one. The coastal south — Maputo, Gaza, Inhambane, down to the Bazaruto coast — is relatively stable, with ordinary crime (petty theft, opportunistic robbery, carjacking risk in Maputo peaking Nov–Mar) as the main concern. The far north (Cabo Delgado, Niassa Special Reserve, northern Nampula) is an active insurgency zone — Level 4 / Do Not Travel — but it is well outside this profile's scope. The pinch point for this route is the central corridor toward Beira.

EN1 / EN6 banditry corridor — central Mozambique Government travel advisories flag armed attacks on vehicles on the EN1 between the Save River and Muxungue, and Gorongosa to Caia, and on the EN6 between Beira and Chimoio (also EN7 Nova Vanduzi–Luenha). Mozambican forces have run military convoys on some stretches. This is the residual of the Renamo-era tension in Sofala/Manica, distinct from the northern insurgency. If the itinerary pushes to Beira, get current local advice, travel only in daylight, and use any organised convoy — or reconsider the inland push and keep to the coast.

09 Health Malaria holoendemic · thin facilities

Southern Mozambique is a year-round malaria area — this is the single biggest health consideration. Prophylaxis is strongly advised, worst risk in the wet season and at the coast. Public medical facilities are weak and often short of supplies; serious care means evacuation, in practice back to South Africa, which puts the R29 medevac cover front and centre on the very first leg.

10 Camp Safety & Wildlife Coastal camps · elephants in parks

Most of the coastal south is settled and farmed rather than big-game wilderness, so camping is generally in established beach camps and lodges rather than wild bush. The main wildlife hazards are concentrated in the parks: elephants (Maputo NP has a substantial coastal population), hippos and crocodiles at lakes and rivers, and marine hazards on the coast.

12 Money & Currency Metical · cash country · ATMs in towns

The currency is the Mozambican metical (MZN / MT). Cards work in Maputo hotels, larger supermarkets and some coastal lodges, but Mozambique is fundamentally a cash economy once off the main tourist track — carry meticais for fuel (often), tolls, market food, camp fees and checkpoints. ATMs (Visa/Mastercard/Maestro) are in the towns along the EN1; some cap withdrawal amounts per transaction.

13 National Parks & Camping Maputo NP · Limpopo NP · Bazaruto · Gorongosa

Southern Mozambique's protected areas are a mix of rewilded terrestrial parks and marine sanctuaries. Fees are modest and generally paid in cash at the gate; confirm current rates on the managing body's site before travel rather than trusting a figure that dates quickly.

Terrestrial · self-drive

Maputo National Park

  • ~1 hr south of Maputo via the Katembe bridge + EN200 to Futi Gate.
  • Merged 2021 (Special Reserve + Ponta do Ouro Marine). Peace Parks–managed.
  • Elephants, zebra, giraffe, hippo; turtle nesting Oct–Mar. Sandy tracks — 4×4, deflate to ~1 bar (posted at gate).
  • Day & overnight; community lodge + camping. Easy, rewarding first park.
Terrestrial · TFCA

Limpopo National Park

  • Entered via Giriyondo (from Kruger) or Massingir. Part of Great Limpopo TFCA.
  • Wilder, rougher tracks; fuel/supplies limited — self-sufficient.
  • Corridor between Kruger and the Mozambican interior.
Marine · boat access

Bazaruto Archipelago NP

  • Off Vilanculos / Inhassoro (EN1 turnoff at Pambara). African Parks–managed.
  • Dugong, whale sharks, turtles, reefs. Access by boat/dhow only — no camping on the islands; base in Vilanculos.
  • The vehicle stays mainland; this is a boat day, not a drive.
Terrestrial · Sofala

Gorongosa NP

  • Flagship rewilding park in Sofala — but the EN1 approach runs through the banditry corridor (§08).
  • Only with current security advice and daylight/convoy discipline.
  • Also nearby: Pomene Reserve (coastal, Inhambane) and Zinave NP (inland rewilding).
Fees & booking Park entry + vehicle fees are payable at the gate (cash); Maputo NP publishes rates at parquemaputo.gov.mz. Island access to Bazaruto is via licensed operators from Vilanculos. Verify current fees and gate hours close to travel.

17 Far South Route Guide Border → Maputo → Maputo NP → Ponta

Entry & the far south

Lebombo / Kosi Bay · 3–5 days · easy shakedown

A relaxed opening leg that doubles as the vehicle's shakedown: cross, sort documents and SORCA, provision in Maputo, then sample the far-south coast and the elephants of Maputo NP before committing north.

  • Cross at Lebombo/Ressano Garcia (tar, fast) or Kosi Bay/Ponta (sand, scenic) — SORCA + TIP + gate pass.
  • Maputo: SIM, meticais, fuel, provisions, Toyota check if wanted. Shake down the fresh LiFePO4 + injection work on shore power.
  • Maputo NP (Futi Gate): deflate to ~1 bar, elephants + coast, overnight camp/lodge.
  • Ponta do Ouro / Malongane: diving, dolphins; refuel discipline (arrive full).
  • Debrief the shakedown against R23 before heading up the EN1.
18 Coastal Route Guide — Maputo to the Bazaruto Coast EN1 spine · Xai-Xai → Inhambane → Vilanculos

Up the EN1 coastal spine

Maputo → Vilanculos · ~700 km · 5–8 days at coast pace

The core of the profile: the coastal belt, hopping between beach camps with the EN1 as the spine and sand tracks out to the sea.

  • Maputo → Xai-Xai (~220 km): EN1; fuel at Macia/Xai-Xai; first coastal camps.
  • Xai-Xai → Inhambane/Tofo (~300 km): via Quissico & Maxixe; deflate for Tofo/Barra sand.
  • Inhambane → Vilanculos (~250 km): via Massinga; Bazaruto boat day from Vilanculos; Toyota/VES on hand.
  • Keep both aux cells full leaving Maputo and Vilanculos; camp by mid-afternoon.
Northbound to Malawi — forward note (out of scope here). Beyond Inhassoro the EN1 runs to the Save River and the §08 banditry corridor toward Beira, and the practical route to Malawi turns inland via the Tete corridor (central Mozambique) — terrain deliberately not covered in profile 007. That leg, and the choice of corridor and licence-break timing, are carried forward to the Malawi profile (008 / EX-5) and the route & licence-break workshop (EX-9). Do not treat the Beira/Sofala push as routine: it is a security decision taken on current advice.
