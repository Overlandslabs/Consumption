❌ NO RECORD — Cheap part, easy fit. Top-3 cause of 1HZ overheating when stuck closed.

A stuck-closed thermostat causes rapid overheating; a stuck-open thermostat slows warm-up and reduces heater performance. Either failure mode is unacceptable on a turbocharged 1HZ. The 82°C OEM thermostat is the correct part — do not fit lower-temperature variants on this engine.

Action: Replace at next workshop visit. Combine with coolant flush + pressure test. Carry the removed unit as a field spare.

2 · WHEEL BEARING REPACK

⚠️ Bearings replaced Feb 2025 @ 271,200km. Repack interval is 20,000km — last repack ~245,000km. Approximately 63,684km overdue on repack interval.

The bearings are new, but Toyota requires repacking (not replacement) every 20,000km in severe-use conditions to refresh the grease. Old grease loses lubricating properties and traps moisture/grit, which accelerates bearing wear.

Action: Schedule front + rear wheel bearing repack. Use Toyota-spec wheel bearing grease. Inspect seals at the same time. Combine with brake inspection.

3 · DIFF BREATHER EXTENSIONS — VERIFY

Standard Toyota diff breathers are inadequate for water crossings deeper than ankle height. Extended breathers route front diff, rear diff, and transfer case vents to a high point in the engine bay. Cheap and simple — but only if not already fitted.

§4Service HighlightsAPR 2026 @ 308,248 KM
⚠️ 1 · Rear Hub Seal Leak — Apr 2026
Hub seal weeping — risk of oil tracking onto rear brake shoes. Shoes replaced Jan 2026 @ 293,949km.

⚠️ Rear hub seal weeping · 308,248km

The rear axle hub seals retain gear oil in the axle housing. A weeping seal allows oil to track onto the brake drum and rear brake shoes. On the LC76, the rear drums are the primary parking brake — oil-contaminated shoes lose friction coefficient completely.

Action: Check both rear drums for oil contamination before the expedition. A wet shoe requires immediate hub seal replacement and new shoes — do not drive. Confirm which side is leaking and monitor for progression. Rear brake shoes were replaced Jan 2026 @ 293,949km — contamination of those shoes within 14,299km would be a fast failure.

⚠️ 2 · Fat Knuckle Grease Leak — Apr 2026
Front knuckle grease escaping — birfield runs dry without grease. Knuckle kit replaced Feb 2025 @ 271,200km.

⚠️ Front knuckle grease escaping · 308,248km

The front axle birfield joints (constant velocity joints) on the LC76 run inside the steering knuckle housing, packed with grease. The knuckle seal retains this grease. Loss of grease means the birfield runs dry — once it is dry, wear accelerates rapidly and CV failure follows. The front knuckle/spindle overhaul kit was replaced Feb 2025 @ 271,200km — 37,048km ago.

Action: Confirm which side is leaking. Re-pack the knuckle with correct birfield grease (Molybdenum disulphide grease) and inspect the knuckle seal for damage. If the seal face is damaged, replace the seal before the expedition. Do not leave this for the field — a dry birfield in deep sand or river crossings accelerates to failure quickly.

🔩 4 · Rear Propshaft Dust Cover — Minor Grease Weep — 2026
Owner daily-check finding. Slip-joint dust cover on the rear propshaft weeping a little grease and protruding slightly. No noise, vibration, or other symptom.

⚠️ Observation: The dust cover over the rear propshaft sliding spline (slip joint) is weeping a small amount of grease and protrudes ever so slightly. No noises, vibration, or anything else unusual noticed.

The dust cap and seal over the slip joint retain the spline grease and keep grit out. A slight protrusion means the slip joint is sitting a little further extended than before — consistent with the rear ride-height change from the OME leaf-spring set fitted Jan 2026 @ 294,500km, which alters the propshaft working length. A minor grease weep here is common and usually benign, but a displaced or split boot lets the spline pick up grit — the same contamination path that shortens UJ life (see item 4).

Action: Monitor — not urgent in the absence of noise or vibration. At the next service have the dust cover/boot inspected and reseated, the spline re-greased, and confirm no grit ingress or play. Keep the area clean and watch for increasing protrusion, a torn boot, or any new clunk on torque take-up.

🔊 5 · Low-RPM Gear Rattle — Confirmed Benign — 2026
Light clattering at low RPM in gear under light load. Diagnosed as benign gear rattle from the Terrain Tamer 4WD HD clutch damper — not a fault. Full diagnosis in R13.

✓ Diagnosis: Gear rattle. The Terrain Tamer 4WD HD clutch (fitted Feb 2026 @ 296,500km) uses stiffer torsion-damper springs than a standard clutch, so it passes more of the 1HZ's torsional pulses into the gearbox. At low RPM under light load — where net torque hovers near zero — the unloaded gears jiggle across their tooth backlash and rattle. It is a known characteristic of the HD clutch, not a defect, and serves as an audible lugging cue (change down or ease off).

Confirmed by the clutch-dip test: rolling in gear with the noise present, dipping the clutch pedal (decoupling the engine) stops the rattle instantly; the vehicle is silent in neutral at idle. That signature isolates it to engine-pulse-driven gear rattle.

Ruled out: valve-clearance clatter (would track engine RPM regardless of gear/load and be present in neutral — it is silent in neutral); release/throw-out bearing (would change or worsen with the pedal pressed in — it vanishes); driveline UJ (would track road speed as a clunk on take-up, not a continuous rattle).

Action: None required — awareness only. Re-run the clutch-dip test if the character changes (appears in neutral, tracks road speed, or worsens with the pedal in). Full field-diagnosis write-up in R13 Drivetrain Training.

§8Critical Inspections & Red FlagsSTOP-DRIVING CONDITIONS
⚠️ Daily/Weekly Visual Checks
Regular visual checks prevent catastrophic failures.

Daily/Weekly:

□ Viscous fan (should NOT spin freely when hot)
□ Boost leaks (listen for hissing)
□ Oil leaks (turbo, rocker cover, rear main seal)
□ Coolant level
□ EGT readings under load

Every 10,000km:

□ Blow-by check (oil filler cap @ idle)
□ Turbo shaft play
□ Intercooler boots (oil sweating)
□ Air intake (smoke test)

🚨 Red Flag Conditions — STOP DRIVING
These symptoms indicate imminent failure. Continued driving causes catastrophic damage.

STOP IMMEDIATELY if you observe:

❌ Rising EGT under same load • ❌ Sudden coolant loss • ❌ White smoke (head gasket) • ❌ Excessive black smoke • ❌ Loss of power + high temps • ❌ Metallic engine noises
