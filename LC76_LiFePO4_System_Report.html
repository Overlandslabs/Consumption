Section 01
System Overview & Layout

This is the electrical system as installed (Manjaro Industries, quote LC76SW004). It is dual-chemistry: an Enertec Power+ LiFePO4 house battery for accessories, kept entirely separate from the vehicle's Willard 650 lead-acid crank battery. All charging and control electronics are Victron, monitored in VictronConnect; the house battery is monitored in its own Bluetooth BMS app (Bms Blue Tooth).

⚠ 105 A discharge ceiling — the hard load budget
The Enertec BMS limits continuous discharge to 105 A. That is the total continuous 12 V load budget. The MultiPlus at full inverter draw pulls ≈ 40 A DC, leaving ~65 A for all DC loads at once. Size high-draw kit against this ceiling — the BMS will disconnect if it is exceeded.
Earthing — non-isolated, common negative
The Orion DC-DC is a non-isolated unit: input and output share the battery negative. The system therefore uses a single clean common-negative star-earth — no galvanic isolation, one earth reference for the house electronics and the SmartShunt. The DC-DC output is capped to ~40 A in VictronConnect to protect the 1HZ alternator.

Section 03
Battery Longevity & Care

LiFePO4 (LFP) chemistry is tolerant of Southern African heat and cycles happily to 80% depth of discharge day after day, with no memory effect and no need to sit at 100%. At ~77 Ah/day this system runs about 37% daily DoD — a moderate, low-stress zone. The notes below are absolute LFP behaviour for the fitted 210 Ah battery.

Cycle Life & the Use-Pattern Reality

Enertec rates the Power+ at >3,000 cycles at 80% DoD (0.5C, 25°C); because this system runs at only ~37% daily DoD, real cycle life runs higher still. Under Southern African heat a conservative field expectation is around 2,400 cycles → ~6–7 years at full-time daily cycling (modelled below). But cycle ageing only accumulates when you actually cycle the battery — so for realistic use, calendar ageing (~15–20 years) is the limit, and the battery outlasts the vehicle.

This models full-time daily cycling. At any realistic intermittent pattern the battery reaches calendar end-of-life long before this cycle limit.

Depth of discharge
LFP cycles comfortably to 80% DoD daily; occasional deeper draws are fine. There is no memory effect and no benefit to routinely charging to 100% and holding — partial-state cycling is healthy. Let the SmartShunt SoC guide you, not lead-acid habits.
Temperature
Discharge is unaffected across the operating heat range. Charging below ~0°C is blocked by the internal BMS to protect the cells — the BMS handles this automatically, no user action needed. Heat accelerates ageing modestly (already baked into the SA de-rate). Keep the battery out of direct sun and reasonably ventilated.
Long-term storage
Between trips, store the house battery at roughly 50–60% SoC in a cool, dry place — this maximises calendar life. Avoid leaving it long-term at full charge or near-empty. Check SoC every few months and top up toward the 50–60% band if it has drifted low.
