01 Entry & Visas SA Passport · Visa-Free 90 Days

South African passport holders enter Botswana visa-free for up to 90 days — no advance application, no fee, stamp issued on arrival at all recognised entry points. Botswana is one of the most straightforward border crossings in southern Africa.

02 Border Crossings From SA · From Namibia · Into Zambia / Zimbabwe

Botswana shares borders with South Africa (south), Namibia (west and north — Caprivi Strip), Zimbabwe (east), and Zambia (north — Kazungula). For the LC76 expedition routing south to north, the most likely entries are from Namibia at Ngoma Bridge (Caprivi east), and exit north to Zambia at Kazungula. From SA, the main northern entry is Tlokweng/Pioneer Gate near Gaborone.

FMD Vet Cordon — Foot and Mouth Disease Botswana has a network of veterinary cordon fences separating livestock zones from wildlife areas. When crossing these fences — which you will cross multiple times on a northern Botswana circuit — you must not carry raw red meat, raw dairy products, or soil-contaminated items northward through the fence. Freeze-dried and vacuum-sealed processed meat is acceptable. This is a serious livestock biosecurity measure — violations carry fines. Know where the cordon fences are on your route and plan provisions accordingly. The main cordon relevant to this route is the Buffalo Fence running south of the Okavango Delta and Moremi.

Makgadikgadi timing note The Makgadikgadi offers two completely different experiences: the dry-season mineral pans (blinding white salt, meerkats, brown hyena, Baines' Baobabs) and the wet-season Boteti River corridor (zebra migration November–April — up to 30,000 Burchell's zebra). CKGR fuel planning — critical The Central Kalahari Game Reserve is one of the world's largest protected areas (52,800 km²). Interior circuits on deep sand significantly increase fuel consumption — plan at 14–15L/100km on sand, not 12L. Do the arithmetic before committing.

06 Roads Left-Hand Traffic · Deep Sand · 4×4 Essential

Botswana drives on the left — same as South Africa, which makes the transition from Namibia easy. The main sealed arteries (A1 Gaborone–Francistown–Nata, A3 Trans-Kalahari Highway, A33 Nata–Kasane) are generally good condition. The national parks are a completely different story: deep Kalahari sand, flooded pans, and sandy riverbeds define the experience. A 4×4 with low-range and sand recovery gear is non-negotiable for any national park entry.

07 Security Very Safe · Standard Urban Awareness

Botswana is one of the safest countries in Africa for overlanders. Politically stable, prosperous by regional standards (diamonds), low violent crime rate, professional and non-corrupt police. The primary security risks in Botswana are urban opportunist crime in Gaborone and Francistown, road safety (livestock and wildlife), and the isolation hazard in remote national parks.

Security Positives

  • Consistently rated one of Africa's safest destinations
  • Police professional and generally not corrupt
  • Rural areas: zero documented armed crime against overlanders
  • National parks: human security risk is essentially nil
  • No conflict history, stable democracy since 1966
  • BDF (Botswana Defence Force) maintains strong anti-poaching presence in parks

Genuine Risks

  • Gaborone: vehicle break-ins, bag snatching in busy commercial areas
  • Road safety: donkeys and cattle on main roads at dusk/dawn — serious collision risk
  • Elephant and lion on roads at night in Chobe District — even on tarmac
  • Remote park isolation: CKGR breakdown means days before help — satellite comms essential
  • Flash floods in CKGR and Makgadikgadi can strand vehicles rapidly in wet season

09 Camp Safety & Wildlife Ground Tent · Unfenced · Elephant · Lion · Hyena

Botswana's national park campsites are unfenced and in the middle of active wildlife habitat. This is the defining character of the Botswana experience — and the reason the camps are so extraordinary. The rules are different from anything in fenced South African parks. Every camp in Chobe, Moremi, and the CKGR must be treated as active lion and elephant territory at all times.

Snakes & Smaller Hazards

Botswana has puff adders, black mamba, Mozambique spitting cobra, boomslang, and various smaller species. The puff adder risk is greatest in the grassy, rocky margins of CKGR campsites and Makgadikgadi surrounds. Spitting cobras are more prevalent in the wetter northern parks. Shake boots every morning. Scorpions are present throughout — particularly in the Kalahari and pan environments. Check sleeping gear and footwear. Tsetse flies are present in northern Botswana (Chobe, Okavango region) — wear cover and use DEET. Avoid dark blue/black clothing in fly zones.

11 Money & Currency Pula · ZAR Accepted · Multi-Currency Gates

Botswana uses the Pula (BWP). Unusually, park gates accept multiple currencies — Pula, South African Rand, USD, and GBP — making Botswana logistics easier than Zambia. ZAR is widely accepted in border towns and at tourist facilities throughout northern Botswana. Pula cash is ideal; ZAR works as backup everywhere.

12 Campsite Booking System Five Operators · DWNP · Multi-Step Process

Botswana's campsite booking system is the most complex logistical challenge in planning the trip. Since 2009, most national park campsites are managed by four private operators rather than DWNP directly. Park entry fees remain separate and are paid to DWNP. You will deal with up to five different entities for a single park visit. Book 6–12 months ahead for any July–September dates. May–June: 3–4 months sufficient.

Do not arrive without a confirmed campsite booking Arriving at a Botswana national park gate without a pre-confirmed campsite reservation will result in being turned away — particularly at weekends and peak season when booking offices are closed. This is not a guideline; it is standard DWNP procedure. Plan and book before arrival.

Step-by-Step Booking Process

For a mixed-park circuit (Chobe + Moremi + Makgadikgadi)
  • Step 1: Decide your route and dates. Identify all campsites needed with GPS coordinates.
  • Step 2: Check DWNP availability first for any DWNP-managed sites (tel/email above). Reserve provisionally — do not pay yet.
  • Step 3: Email all private operators simultaneously (Kwalate / SKL / Xomae / BigFoot as relevant). State: dates, number of adults, vehicle registration, SA passport.
  • Step 4: Once private operator confirms availability, pay private operators and obtain booking vouchers.
  • Step 5: Send all booking vouchers to DWNP (dwnp@gov.bw or fax +267 318 0775). DWNP will then invoice you for park entry permit fees.
  • Step 6: Pay DWNP park entry fees. Receive entry permits.
  • Step 7: Print ALL vouchers and permits. No cell service inside parks — digital-only is not reliable at gates.
  • Step 8 (optional): Pay park entry fees in cash at the gate on arrival — this is possible if you have campsite vouchers confirmed, though pre-paying avoids any gate-day complications.
Maun as your booking base If you have not pre-booked and arrive in Maun without reservations, the DWNP Maun office (S19.98°, E23.43°) and private operator Maun offices can sometimes sort last-minute availability — but do not count on it in peak season. Maun-based agents (McKenzie 4×4, Travel Adventures Botswana, Go Bold Safaris) can handle all bookings for you for a small fee — worth it if the admin complexity of the five-operator system is daunting.
13 Kalahari Region CKGR · Makgadikgadi · Nxai Pan · Kgalagadi

The Kalahari is not a desert in the conventional sense — it is a fossil sand sea, ancient Aeolian dunes now stabilised by vegetation, sitting on a vast underground water table. The ecosystem is one of Africa's most extraordinary: sparse, seemingly empty, then suddenly revealing lions, brown hyena, black-maned Kalahari lions, oryx, springbok, and vast skies that dwarf everything. The Central Kalahari Game Reserve alone is 52,800 km² — larger than Switzerland.

Central Kalahari Game Reserve (CKGR)52,800 km² · Expert 4×4 · Remotest Park
★★★★★ Wilderness Absolute

Deception Valley — CKGR Heartland

  • Ancient fossil valley carved by the (now dry) Deception River — 3.5 billion years old in parts
  • The Kalahari's famous black-maned lions — the males grow unusually long, dark manes due to the harsh climate. One of the most distinctive lion populations in Africa.
  • Cheetah on the open valley floor — exceptional sightings in the early morning
  • Brown hyena: nocturnal, shy, and rarely seen elsewhere — CKGR gives some of the best encounters
  • Oryx (gemsbok), springbok, eland, wildebeest — large herds in the dry season
  • Deception Valley campsite (BigFoot): sits right in the valley — completely isolated, extraordinary night sky, remarkable silence broken only by lions calling
  • Access: Matswere Gate (east, near Rakops) or Xade Gate (west, near Ghanzi). Matswere most used.
  • Road from Matswere to Deception Valley: ~130 km of deep Kalahari sand. Allow 4–5 hours. Low range required for significant stretches.
★★★★ Remote Interior

Sunday Pan & Passarge Valley

  • Further south in CKGR — even more remote than Deception Valley
  • Sunday Pan: open saltpan, flat, stark. Oryx and springbok in large numbers. Excellent for predator sightings at the pan edges at dawn and dusk.
  • Passarge Valley: similar character to Deception but less visited — more likelihood of having the entire landscape to yourself
  • Both managed by BigFoot Tours — book as part of a Deception Valley circuit
  • Self-rescue essential: 300+ km from any town, no comms, no other vehicles guaranteed
★★★★ Easier Access

Khutse Game Reserve

  • Contiguous with CKGR to the south — managed separately but accessed as part of Kalahari circuit
  • Only 240 km from Gaborone — the most accessible Kalahari destination from SA
  • Brown hyena, lion, cheetah, wild dog occasional. Less dramatic landscape than Deception Valley but excellent and less-visited.
  • All five campsites managed by BigFoot Tours
  • DWNP park entry fees apply separately from CKGR
Makgadikgadi PansWorld's Largest Salt Pan System
★★★★★ Iconic

Baines' Baobabs — Nxai Pan

  • A cluster of ancient baobab trees (painted by Thomas Baines in 1862) standing alone on the edge of Nxai Pan — one of the most photographed subjects in Botswana
  • Virtually unchanged in over 160 years — you are standing where Baines stood with his paint and canvas
  • Xomae Adventures manages the campsite directly adjacent — one of the most atmospheric campsites in Africa
  • Access: Via Nxai Pan NP gate (Gweta side) — ~70 km from Gweta. Seasonal: wet-season flooding can block access November–April
  • Dry season: firm salt pan surface, stark white, deeply atmospheric
★★★★★ Wildlife

Makgadikgadi Pans NP — Khumaga & Boteti River

  • The Boteti River runs along the western edge of Makgadikgadi Pans NP — one of the few permanent water sources in the area
  • Zebra migration (November–April): 25,000–30,000 Burchell's zebra follow the rains from the pan basin to the Boteti River corridor — the second largest zebra migration in Africa after the Serengeti
  • Dry season (June–October): elephants, lions, and predators concentrated at the Boteti River — excellent game viewing from Khumaga camp
  • Khumaga camp (SKL) sits directly on the Boteti River — excellent hippo and elephant viewing from camp
  • Meerkats at Makgadikgadi: habituated meerkat families near Ntwetwe Pan — a famous experience where meerkats use you as a lookout post
★★★★ Salt Pans

Ntwetwe Pan — Flamingos & Meerkats

  • Vast blinding white salt surface — disorienting and spectacular in all directions
  • Flamingos gather on the shallow alkaline water after rains — pink against white salt
  • Habituated meerkat families: contact Jack's Camp or Planet Baobab for meerkat sunrise experiences — early morning dawn ritual of meerkats warming themselves on an open observer's arm or head
  • Quad biking on the pan surface is available through Planet Baobab
  • Star gazing from the flat pan: zero light pollution, 360° horizon — extraordinary
Kgalagadi Transfrontier ParkSouthern Kalahari · SA Botswana Shared

The Kgalagadi Transfrontier Park (shared with South Africa's Kgalagadi section) occupies the far south-western corner of Botswana at the convergence of the Nossob and Auob dry riverbeds. While primarily accessed from the SA side, the Botswana (Mabuasehube) section offers a more remote experience with basic campsites in a completely undeveloped environment. Black-maned Kalahari lions, leopards, cheetah, and raptors are the signature species. Mabuasehube Pan is one of the most isolated camping experiences in southern Africa. Managed by DWNP (southern sector) and Intertourism Group for private camps. Access from Tsabong in the far south-west, or crossing from the SA KGNP main camp area. Road from Tsabong to Mabuasehube is deep sand — 4×4 expert territory.

14 Okavango Delta & Chobe Moremi · Chobe · Savuti · Khwai

Northern Botswana is where the Okavango River fans out into an inland delta of 15,000 km² — one of the world's seven natural wonders — and where the Chobe River system supports the highest elephant density on Earth. This is the classic Botswana safari zone: extraordinary wildlife, dramatic landscapes, and some of the most sought-after campsites in Africa.

— Chobe National Park11,700 km² · Highest Elephant Density on Earth
★★★★★ Must-Do

Chobe Riverfront — Kasane & Ihaha

  • The Chobe River supports an estimated 130,000 elephants — the largest concentration in the world. Seeing 200+ elephants at the river simultaneously is a routine afternoon experience.
  • Boat cruises on the Chobe River: the most iconic way to see the elephants and buffalo herds — book from Kasane (multiple operators, roughly BWP 400/person)
  • Ihaha campsite (Kwalate): directly on the Chobe River — elephants drink at the bank metres from camp. One of the most spectacular camping positions in Africa.
  • Kasane town has all services — fuel, supermarkets, ATM, accommodation
  • Chobe riverfront game drive: gravel road with deep sand sections along the river — use 4×4 throughout
★★★★★ Legendary

Savuti — Lions & Elephants

  • The Savuti Channel — a mysterious watercourse that has flowed, stopped, and reversed multiple times in recorded history — creates a wildlife oasis deep in Chobe's interior
  • The Savuti lion prides are famous for hunting elephants — the only lion population consistently documented doing this. Seeing a pride stalk or take down an elephant is one of Africa's rarest wildlife encounters.
  • Rock paintings at Gubatsa Hills — San bushman rock art dating back 3,000+ years
  • Savuti campsite (SKL): set in open woodland near the channel — lions walk through camp regularly. Hyenas are bold, persistent, and intelligent camp raiders — secure everything.
  • Access from Kasane: ~180 km on deep sand tracks. Allow a full day. Fill fuel in Kasane — nothing available at Savuti.
★★★★ Remote

Linyanti Wetlands

  • Northwest Chobe — the Linyanti River system creates a remote wetland bordering Namibia's Caprivi Strip
  • Wild dog: some of Botswana's best wild dog sightings — packs use the Linyanti as a denning area
  • Red lechwe, sitatunga, elephant, lion — wetland-edge species
  • SKL Linyanti camp: most remote public campsite in Chobe NP — a serious self-sufficient expedition
  • Access from Kasane or from the Namibia Caprivi side via Ngoma
— Moremi Game Reserve & KhwaiHeart of the Okavango Delta
★★★★★ Best Camp in Moremi

Third Bridge — Moremi

  • A simple wooden bridge over a narrow Okavango channel, surrounded by palm islands and papyrus — one of the most beautiful campsites in Africa
  • Hippo pods directly adjacent to camp — the night sounds of hippos grunting in the channel are unforgettable
  • Lions and leopard patrol the camp regularly. A leopard den is located within 2 km — habitually active
  • The famous Moremi hippo path crosses the campsite — hippos return to the water at dawn along a path that runs through the tent area. Do not intercept a hippo on its path.
  • Managed by Xomae Adventures — book months ahead. The most sought-after public campsite in Moremi.
  • Road from South Gate to Third Bridge: ~50 km of deep sand, seasonal water crossings. Allow 3–4 hours.
★★★★★ Wild Dog Capital

Xakanaxa & Moremi Tongue

  • At the end of the Moremi Tongue — the deep sand peninsula that juts into the heart of the delta
  • Xakanaxa Lagoon: hippo pods, crocodiles, and extraordinary waterbird diversity — African fish eagle, saddlebill stork, open-billed stork, pygmy goose
  • Wild dog packs den near Xakanaxa — this is one of the most reliable wild dog locations in Africa, particularly April–June when pups are present at the den
  • Elephant and buffalo herds throughout. Lions common.
  • Kwalate Safaris manages Xakanaxa campsite — same responsiveness caveats apply; follow up diligently
★★★★ Community Area

Khwai Community Area

  • The Khwai Community Area borders Moremi GR to the north — wildlife moves freely between both areas
  • Community-managed concession: slightly different rules from the NP but same wildlife. Night drives are permitted here (unlike inside Moremi itself)
  • SKL Khwai North Gate campsite: on the Khwai River — excellent elephant and hippo viewing
  • The Khwai River floodplain is superb game viewing — lion, wild dog, elephant, and red lechwe all present
  • Night drives reveal civets, porcupine, African wildcat, and frequent lion and leopard encounters
— Other HighlightsBeyond the Big Parks
★★★★ Accessible

Khama Rhino Sanctuary

  • Community-run sanctuary 25 km from Serowe — the most accessible rhino viewing in Botswana
  • White and black rhino in a semi-wild setting. The sanctuary was established in 1992 when rhino were locally extinct — now over 30 animals.
  • Camping on-site at the sanctuary. Very affordable. No 4×4 required for the sanctuary itself.
  • GPS: ~S22.65°, E26.72°. On the road between Gaborone and Francistown — natural stopover on the north–south route.
★★★★ Culture

Tsodilo Hills

  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: 4,500 rock paintings on four hills in the far northwest, near Shakawe on the Namibia border
  • The San people consider the hills a sacred site — they call it the "Mountain of the Gods." The paintings span 100,000 years of human presence.
  • Walking trail to the male, female, and child hills. A local guide is strongly recommended (and often mandatory).
  • Access from Shakawe or Sehithwa on the main road — 30 km on sandy track. 4×4 needed.
  • Basic campsite at the foot of the hills. Carry all water.
★★★ Gateway Town

Maun — Okavango Hub

  • The gateway to the Okavango Delta — bush pilot capital of Africa. Dozens of light aircraft operations flying into delta camps daily.
  • All services: fuel, supermarkets (Choppies, Spar), ATM, mechanical (several Toyota-experienced workshops), accommodation range from budget camping to good lodges
  • Ideal admin base: book final provisions, draw Pula, confirm bookings, service vehicle before Moremi or CKGR circuit
  • Boat trips into the delta periphery available from Maun — can access parts of the delta without flying
  • Crocodile Camp and Island Safari Lodge are popular overlander-friendly options

Botswana Delivers

  • Best elephant and predator concentrations in the world
  • Wild dogs — some of Africa's most reliable sightings in Moremi/Khwai
  • Black-maned Kalahari lions — unique to this region
  • Savuti lion–elephant dynamic — incomparable anywhere else
  • Makgadikgadi / Baines' Baobabs — truly otherworldly landscape
  • Botswana is politically stable, affluent, and professionally managed
  • Multi-currency at park gates (ZAR accepted) — logistics simpler than Zambia
  • SA passport visa-free 90 days — zero entry admin

Genuine Challenges

  • Booking complexity — five operators, multi-step DWNP process
  • Park fees are significant — budget carefully before committing
  • Peak season (Jul–Sep) campsites impossible to get without 6–12 month lead time
  • Deep sand throughout — vehicle preparation and recovery kit non-negotiable
  • CKGR isolation — mechanical failure could mean days stranded without help
  • No cell coverage in any major park — full satellite comms dependency
  • FMD vet cordon complicates meat provisioning for northern parks

16 Southern Botswana Route Guide Entry from SA · Kalahari · Makgadikgadi · Approach to Maun

The southern route enters Botswana from South Africa or from Namibia's western border (Mamuno), traverses the Kalahari, and works north toward Maun as the staging point for the northern parks circuit. This is the less-visited, more challenging, and deeply rewarding part of Botswana that most safaris skip entirely.

Route A — Full Kalahari Circuit (10–14 days)

Tlokweng (SA) or Mamuno (Namibia) → CKGR → Makgadikgadi → Maun
  • Day 1: Cross into Botswana. Head to Khama Rhino Sanctuary near Serowe (~360 km from Tlokweng) — overnight at the sanctuary. Rhino walk in the afternoon.
  • Day 2: Serowe → Rakops → Matswere Gate (CKGR entry). ~260 km. Fill fuel completely at Rakops — last station before CKGR. Arrive late afternoon, set up at Matswere Gate area or first CKGR camp.
  • Days 3–5: CKGR — Deception Valley, Sunday Pan. Morning lion patrols. Midday rest. Evening predator watch. Extraordinary night sky. Completely self-sufficient. Inform BigFoot Tours of your entry date.
  • Day 6: Exit CKGR via Matswere Gate → Rakops → Nata (~350 km). Refuel at Nata. Overnight in Nata area.
  • Day 7: Nata → Gweta (fuel) → Makgadikgadi Pans NP entry → Khumaga campsite on the Boteti River (~180 km). Afternoon game drive along Boteti River.
  • Days 8–9: Makgadikgadi: Khumaga morning drives, afternoon Boteti River walk. Day trip to Baines' Baobabs (Nxai Pan NP via Gweta) — one of Africa's most photogenic spots. Night at Khumaga.
  • Day 10: Khumaga → Maun (~200 km) — provisioning, ATM, vehicle check, confirm northern parks bookings. Overnight in Maun (Crocodile Camp / Island Safari Lodge).

Route B — Kalahari Fast (5–6 days)

For tighter schedules — CKGR highlight + Makgadikgadi day
  • Enter at Tlokweng, bypass Gaborone, head directly north to Rakops
  • 2 nights at Deception Valley CKGR (minimum Kalahari experience)
  • Exit CKGR → Nata → Gweta → Baines' Baobabs day visit → Maun
  • Skip Khumaga if time is tight — Baines' Baobabs via day trip from Gweta is accessible without camping in the park

17 Northern Parks Route Guide Maun → Moremi → Khwai → Chobe → Savuti → Kasane

The northern circuit is the classic Botswana overland route — leaving Maun through Moremi, exiting at Khwai North Gate, passing through the Chobe corridor to Savuti, and ending at Kasane. This is the route with the extraordinary wildlife encounters, the most challenging sand tracks, and the most sought-after campsites in southern Africa.

Route C — Full Northern Circuit (10–12 days from Maun)

Maun → South Gate → Third Bridge → Xakanaxa → Khwai → Savuti → Chobe Riverfront → Kasane
  • Day 1: Maun → South Gate (Moremi entry) → Third Bridge (50 km, allow 3–4 hours on deep sand). Afternoon camp setup. Evening hippo chorus begins.
  • Days 2–3: Third Bridge area: morning game drives, afternoon rest in the heat, night sounds of the delta. Lion and leopard patrol camp nightly.
  • Day 4: Third Bridge → Xakanaxa (~40 km, deep sand). Afternoon — Xakanaxa Lagoon hippos and waterbirds.
  • Days 5–6: Xakanaxa: early morning wild dog den visit (seasonal), game drives on the Moremi Tongue, afternoon lagoon birding. Night — lions calling from the mopane woodland.
  • Day 7: Xakanaxa → Khwai North Gate (exit Moremi, enter Khwai Community Area) → Khwai camp (~30 km). Afternoon floodplain game drive — lechwe, elephant, buffalo.
  • Day 8: Khwai → Savuti (via Chobe NP north boundary, ~150 km deep sand). Full day driving. Arrive Savuti late afternoon. Set up camp and observe the channel area before dark.
  • Days 9–10: Savuti: morning elephant and lion drives, afternoon the famous Savuti channel and rock paintings at Gubatsa Hills. Keep hyenas out of camp — they will take anything.
  • Day 11: Savuti → Kasane via Ngoma (~180 km) or via Chobe riverfront circuit (longer but spectacular). Arrive Kasane — full services.
  • Day 12: Kasane: Chobe boat cruise on the river (morning, book from Kasane town), afternoon rest. Cross Kazungula Bridge to Zambia next day, or exit Ngoma into Namibia.

18 Essential Setswana Phrases English Official · Setswana Widely Spoken

Botswana's official languages are Setswana and English. English is widely spoken in cities, tourist areas, and by all park and lodge staff. At rural villages, police checkpoints, and traditional communities, Setswana is the first language. A few phrases go a very long way — Batswana people respond warmly to any effort in Setswana. Always open with Dumela before any transaction or request.
