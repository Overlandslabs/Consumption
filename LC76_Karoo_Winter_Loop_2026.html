Trip Overview

A slow winter circle through the heart of the Karoo and Bushmanland — succulent plains, dolerite koppies, dry pans and some of the darkest skies in the country. The route runs almost entirely on gravel, threading the Cederberg and Tankwa first, then arcing north into the Hantam before looping home down through the Upper Karoo. The rhythm is deliberately unhurried: two to four nights at most stops, self-sufficient under canvas, buying food and topping up water as you pass through the towns.

Geology & Fossils — The Floor of an Ancient World

Almost everything you drive across on this trip is the Karoo Supergroup — a single, vast pile of sedimentary rock that covers roughly two-thirds of South Africa. It was laid down over about 120 million years, from around 300 to 180 million years ago (Ma), in a great inland basin while this part of Gondwana drifted off the South Pole. Read from the bottom up, it is the story of a continent thawing out of an ice age, flooding, drying into endless river floodplains, and finally being buried under lava as Gondwana tore apart.

Karoo Supergroup
~300–180 Ma
Covers ~⅔ of South Africa. The flat plains, the layered cuttings and the koppies are all chapters of the same book.
The Great Dying
~252 Ma
Earth's largest mass extinction (~90% of species). The Karoo's Beaufort Group holds the world's best land record of it — a global reference for science.
Dolerite
The koppies
~183-Ma magma squeezed between the layers as sills and dykes. Harder than the mudstone around it, so it caps every flat-topped hill you'll see.
Fossil country
Therapsids
The "mammal-like reptiles" that preceded the dinosaurs are found in extraordinary density in the Beaufort mudstones beneath your wheels.
Reading the layers
Layer (oldest first)AgeWhat it records
Dwyka~300 MaGlacial tillite — ice sheets when this land sat near the South Pole.
Ecca~280 MaBlack shales of a vast inland sea; source of the Karoo's coal and shale gas.
Beaufort~265–250 MaRiver-floodplain mudstones — the fossil reptile beds and the end-Permian extinction record.
Stormberg~230–190 MaSandstones and the pale Clarens "cave sandstone"; early dinosaurs.
Drakensberg basalt~183 MaFlood lavas that buried it all as Gondwana broke up. The dolerite below is its plumbing.
⛰
On the ground: every flat-topped koppie and "ironstone" ridge is dolerite-capped; the open plains between are soft Beaufort mudstone. The fossil footprint surface at Gansfontein near Fraserburg (a short detour from the Sutherland leg) is one of the most accessible windows into this world. Note: Ma = million years ago.
05

Biomes & Flora — Two Karoos

The route crosses the meeting place of two great arid biomes. The Succulent Karoo in the west (the Tankwa, Cederberg fringe and Hantam) is a winter-rainfall world and one of only a handful of arid biodiversity hotspots on Earth — around 6,300 plant species, some 40% of them found nowhere else, and the richest succulent flora on the planet. The Nama-Karoo of the interior (Bushmanland and the Upper Karoo) is a summer-rainfall dwarf shrubland — sparse, grey-green "Karoo bossies" and grass over huge distances, deceptively rich underfoot.

Succulent Karoo
Global hotspot
~6,300 species, ~40% endemic. The only arid-zone hotspot on Earth. Tankwa, Cederberg edge, Hantam.
Nama-Karoo
Dwarf shrubland
Hardy shrublets and grasses across Bushmanland and the Upper Karoo. Built for drought and frost.
Quiver tree
Kokerboom
The aloe that isn't a tree. Groves around Loeriesfontein and Nieuwoudtville; San hollowed the branches for arrow quivers.
Spring flowers
Late Aug–Sept
Rain-dependent daisies, vygies and bulbs. Your timing sits right at the front edge of the display.

Nieuwoudtville, a short hop from Calvinia, is often called the bulb capital of the world for its unmatched density of geophyte species. The Biedouw Valley (Enjo) and the Hantam plains can both flower spectacularly in a good year.

🌼
Flower window: displays open from late August and need recent rain plus sun by mid-morning to perform. Check the seasonal flower line before committing to the Calvinia/Nieuwoudtville day trip — and remember flowers face the sun, so drive with the sun behind you for the best show.
06

Wildlife — Sparse, Specialised, Worth the Patience

Karoo wildlife rewards stillness rather than the big-game checklist. Gemsbok, springbok, kudu, steenbok and klipspringer hold the plains and koppies; after dark the Karoo belongs to bat-eared and Cape fox, aardvark, aardwolf, caracal and porcupine. The birding is genuinely special — this is the global heartland of Africa's larks, with several species found almost nowhere else, plus Verreaux's (black) eagle on the cliffs, blue crane on the plains, and the great bustards and korhaans striding the veld.

Karoo icon
Riverine rabbit
Critically endangered and found only in central-Karoo riverine scrub — one of the world's rarest mammals. Largely nocturnal; a privilege to glimpse.
Birding
Lark capital
Karoo, red, Sclater's and other larks; Ludwig's & kori bustard, Karoo korhaan, pale chanting goshawk, ostrich. Tankwa is a top arid-birding spot.
Reptiles
Tortoise central
The Karoo and Namaqualand have the highest tortoise diversity on Earth. Also puff adder and Cape cobra — watch where you step and reach.
Best viewing
Night drives
Melton Wold runs guided night drives (aardvark, bat-eared fox possible). Dawn and dusk are the active windows everywhere.
🦌
Driving + wildlife = the real risk. Kudu, antelope and stray livestock on unfenced gravel are the most likely hazard of the whole trip, and they move at dusk and dawn. Plan to be in camp before dark and avoid night driving on the gravel — it's the single biggest safety lever you have out here.
07

People & Heritage — Deep Time to Radio Telescopes

The Karoo's human story is as layered as its rock. The San — among the oldest continuous cultures on Earth — left rock paintings in the sandstone of the Cederberg and rock engravings (petroglyphs) pecked into the dark dolerite of the central Karoo. Khoekhoe herders followed, and from the 1700s the trekboers — semi-nomadic Dutch-descended pastoralists — pushed in with fat-tailed sheep, founding the merino-wool culture that still defines the region's towns.

First peoples
San rock art
Paintings in Cederberg sandstone; engravings on central-Karoo dolerite, including near Stuurmansfontein. Irreplaceable.
Settler heritage
Corbelled houses
Domed dry-stone dwellings (klipkoepelhuise) built by 1800s trekboers where there was no timber — unique to the Carnarvon–Williston–Fraserburg triangle. Stuurmansfontein is a preserved one you sleep in.
Town culture
Wool & water
Victorian streetscapes, NG churches and sheep economies — Calvinia, Loxton, Sutherland, Williston. Wupperthal in the Cederberg is a 19th-century Moravian mission.
Modern layer
SKA
The Square Kilometre Array radio telescope is rising near Carnarvon, inside a legally protected radio-quiet zone.
🪨
Rock-art etiquette: never touch, wet, chalk or rub paintings or engravings — skin oils and water destroy them. Visit only with the landowner's permission or a guide, and do not publish or share exact panel locations. These sites survive precisely because they stay quiet.
08

Dark Skies & Astronomy

The Karoo has some of the darkest, clearest skies on Earth — almost no light pollution, very low humidity and high ground. It's why Sutherland hosts the South African Astronomical Observatory (SAAO) and the Southern African Large Telescope (SALT), the largest single optical telescope in the Southern Hemisphere, and why the Square Kilometre Array (SKA) radio telescope sits out near Carnarvon. On this trip the pans and remote camps — Tankwa, Verneukpan, Stuurmansfontein, Merweville — are every bit as dark; you simply look up.

Sutherland
SALT & SAAO
Day tour of the telescopes plus a guided town night-sky session — book both ahead. Winter nights are long and crystal clear (and bitterly cold).
New moon
13 Aug 2026
Darkest skies of the trip — and it falls during your Enjo stay (10–14 Aug). The late Tankwa nights are also dark.
Full moon + eclipse
28 Aug 2026
A partial lunar eclipse is visible from South Africa in the early hours of 28 Aug — during your Soutpoort stay. Bright moon, but a real spectacle.
Sky quality
Among the best
The Milky Way core sits well placed in the August evening sky. A red head-torch and 20 minutes of dark adaption transform the view.
✦
Moon-aware planning: aim your darkest-sky expectations at Enjo and late Tankwa (near new moon, 13 Aug). At Verneukpan (19–22 Aug) the waxing moon sets after midnight, so the pre-dawn hours stay dark. The moon is brightest 24 Aug–1 Sept (Melton Wold through early Merweville), then wanes again for Sutherland. Abbreviations: SAAO = SA Astronomical Observatory; SALT = Southern African Large Telescope; SKA = Square Kilometre Array.

Leg 1 — Cederberg & Tankwa

The opening week, all within easy reach of home and building gently toward Enjo. Leave Cape Town with full water and a full main tank — the R355 into the Tankwa is the longest dry, fuel-less stretch of the whole trip.

3–7 Aug · 4 nights
−32.2462, 20.1635 · SANParks reception 027 341 1927 · book via sanparks.org
Facilities
6 private stands, each with its own kitchen, hot shower and flush toilet. Borehole water is mineral-heavy and smells of sulphur — fine for washing, carry your own drinking water. No power, no shop, no fuel.

From Ceres on the R355 — ~250 km of corrugated gravel, no fuel or services. Drop tyre pressures. The internal park roads to Perdekloof are corrugated in sections; take it slow.

Leg 2 — Hantam & Bushmanland

North out of the Cederberg into the wide, empty Hantam and Bushmanland. This is the remotest stretch — long horizons, tiny towns, and the trip's wildest night out on Verneukpan. Top up fuel and food at Calvinia before pushing on.

4 · Calvinia — Kleinplasie

14–16 Aug · 2 nights
−31.4625, 19.8009 · 079 885 9836 · smallholding just outside town
Facilities
Camping with private braai, toilet and shower per site; level but limited shade. (In-town alternative: Klipwerf, 027 341 1126.) Calvinia has supermarkets, fuel, ATMs — the big resupply.
Explore
Calvinia Museum and the giant post box; Akkerendam Nature Reserve walks above town; day trip to Nieuwoudtville — the "bulb capital", flowers and the waterfall, ~70 km. Late Aug can be peak Hantam flower season.
Note
The Hantam Vleisfees (meat festival) is usually late August — worth checking dates; it fills the town.
Drive in
Enjo → Pakhuis → Calvinia ≈ 150 km / ~2.5 hrs.

5 · Loeriesfontein — Skurweberg Bush Camp

16–18 Aug · 2 nights
−31.0097, 19.4472 · Eric 083 763 4836
Facilities
Only 4 rustic sites under the Skurweberg, among quiver trees and aloes; hot showers; two sites have limited solar power (phone/light charging, no kettles). Overlander-favourite, very helpful host.
Explore
The Fred Turner Windpump Museum in Loeriesfontein — one of only a couple in the world, genuinely worth it. Quiver-tree groves, rock formations, big birdsong-and-sunset country.
Drive in
Calvinia → Loeriesfontein ≈ 60 km / ~45 min.

6 · Brandvlei — Casablanca

18–19 Aug · 1 night
−30.4602, 20.4884 · 066 263 0125 · 41 Charl Cilliers St
Facilities
Quirky overnight stop, friendly host. Brandvlei is the last proper resupply and fuel before Verneukpan — fill up here, buy water and food for the wild-camp days.

A staging night rather than a destination. Stretch the legs around the salt-pan town; it's the gateway to the pan.

7 · Verneukpan — Wild Camp

19–22 Aug · 3 nights
−30.1409, 21.0573 · access via Brandvlei · arrange permission locally
Facilities
None. A vast dry lake bed — fully self-sufficient. No water, no fuel, no toilets, no signal. Carry everything in, pack everything out.
Explore
The 1929 Malcolm Campbell "Bluebird" land-speed-record site and the old measured-mile markers; the surreal flat horizon; and perhaps the darkest, most silent skies of the trip. Walk the pan at sunrise.
Access & safety
The pan sits on private farmland — arrange access and directions through Brandvlei tourism / the managing farm before you go. Never drive onto a damp pan surface (breakthrough risk). Exposed to wind and severe frost; no shelter — set up so the tent is anchored hard and braced for cold.
Drive in
Brandvlei → Verneukpan ≈ 70 km / ~1.5 hrs gravel.

Leg 3 — Upper Karoo & Home

Turning south for the long sweep home through the classic Upper Karoo — corbelled stone houses, historic guest farms, quiet villages and, finally, the cold heights of Sutherland. Stuurmansfontein, Melton Wold, Loxton and Soutpoort cluster close together, so the driving eases off here.

8 · Stuurmansfontein Corbelled House

22–24 Aug · 2 nights
−30.9557, 21.6820 · Charmaine/Piet Botha 072 352 8070 · 30 km off the R63 from Carnarvon
Facilities
A restored 1800s corbelled (klipkoepel) stone house — self-catering, off-grid. Gas fridge and stove, Dover wood stove, cast-iron oven, candles, outdoor shower, "swemgat" and wood-fired jacuzzi, boma. No power, no signal. Bring all food and drinking water.
Explore
San rock art and engravings on the farm; total solitude and silence; heritage interest in the corbelled-house architecture unique to this corner of the Karoo. Detour into Carnarvon for the village and resupply.
Drive in
Verneukpan → Vanwyksvlei → Carnarvon → Stuurmansfontein ≈ 180 km / ~3 hrs gravel.

9 · Melton Wold Guest Farm

24–26 Aug · 2 nights
−31.4573, 22.7530 · 053 621 1912 · on the R63, Loxton–Victoria West
Facilities
10 campsites with water, power and built-in braais under willow trees; swimming pool; clean ablutions; firewood and braai packs for sale. Historic guesthouse and meals on site if you want a night off the camp stove.
Explore
Large working farm with game — self-drive and arranged night drives (bat-eared fox, aardvark possible); walking trails; old-world Karoo homestead atmosphere.
Drive in
Stuurmansfontein → Carnarvon → R63 → Melton Wold ≈ 150 km / ~2 hrs.

10 · Loxton — Pear Tree

26–27 Aug · 1 night
−31.4783, 22.3521 · Alewyn & Anelise 083 411 1035 · cnr Beaufort & Schalkwyk St
Facilities
Small in-town camp with private ablutions and a dish-wash area; friendly hosts. Office is the red-roofed house across the road.
Explore
Loxton is one of the prettiest Karoo villages — pepper trees, restored architecture, quiet streets made for an evening walk or cycle. Good village shop and eateries.
Drive in
Melton Wold → Loxton ≈ 45 km / ~40 min.

Birding, walks and cycling on the farm; cool off in the splash pool with the Karoo plains laid out below. A quiet, remote counterpoint to in-town Loxton.

Fuel & Range

Fuel is comfortable on this trip — the LC76's 145 L main tank plus the 2 × 20 L Desert Fox cells give well over 1,000 km of range, far more than any single leg demands. The discipline is simply to fill at the right towns and never run a tank down before a long gravel gap.

~1,000+ km
Realistic range
~330 km
Longest leg (in to Tankwa)

Reliable fill points: Ceres (before the R355), Calvinia (big resupply), Brandvlei (last before Verneukpan), Carnarvon, Fraserburg, Sutherland, Matjiesfontein/Laingsburg on the run home. No fuel: the R355 corridor, inside Tankwa NP, Verneukpan, and the small farm-camp stops.

Two gaps to respect: fill to the brim in Ceres before the R355, and in Brandvlei before the Verneukpan / Vanwyksvlei stretch. Run the calculators (LC76 Fuel Range / Trip Fuel Planner) against your actual loaded consumption if you want exact margins.

14

Weather & Season

August is deep Karoo winter: dry, clear, superb for gravel and stargazing — and cold. Daytime is pleasant (often 16–22 °C) but nights are hard frost, especially at altitude on the Roggeveld around Sutherland and exposed on Verneukpan, where −5 °C or lower and snow are both possible. Wind on the open pans can be fierce.

❄
Pack for serious cold: proper sleeping bags rated well below freezing, an insulated mat, beanies and a warm layer for the evenings. The OzTent handles wind well but stake and brace it hard at Verneukpan and Sutherland.

The upside: late August is the start of the spring flower season in the Hantam — Nieuwoudtville and the Biedouw Valley (Enjo) can be exceptional if the timing lands. Worth a flower-line check around mid-August before committing to Calvinia day trips.

Gravel & Vehicle Notes

Roughly 80% of this route is gravel — corrugations, loose surfaces and the occasional rocky section, but no serious 4×4 obstacles on the planned line. The LC76 on OME is well within its element; the main wear factors are corrugation fatigue and tyre damage, both managed by pressure and pace.

Tyre pressures
Drop for gravel
Lower from your tar pressures for grip and comfort — run the Tyre Pressure Calculator for the loaded figure. Re-inflate for tar runs (R63, N1).
Pace
Slow on corrugation
The R355 and Tankwa interior are notoriously corrugated. Easy speeds save the vehicle and your fillings.
Recovery / spares
Self-reliant
Long stretches with no signal or traffic. Carry the full spares/tools kit; check the recovery gear before the remote legs.

Watch the usual monitor items between legs — a quick under-vehicle look at each camp for the known seepage points, hubs and propshaft. The corrugated sections are exactly where small leaks show themselves.
